MODULE FUMB

CARD A

Model Generalities

GAS CELL EXCHANGER

 

The FUMB module simulates the functioning of a flue cell whose are only described the heat exchange phenomena from flue gas to the metal of a tube bundle containing the fluid to heat.

The heat transfer occurs both by convection and radiance which respective coefficient (also as a function of flue geometry) are drawn by "STEAM - Its Generation and Use", Babcock and Wilcox, 1963.

Compressibility and head losses phenomena along the duct are not considered. The presence of exchange surfaces different from the tube bundle (enclosures) is taken into account in the flue gas energetic balance considering a transferred power oversized with a factor equal to the ratio between enclosures and tube bundle surfaces.

FUMB must be connected to a piping module representing the tube bundle (typically EVAF module).

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[K] TMTF metal superficial temperature (flue gas side) of the pipe containing the fluid to heat
[K] TFIC flue temperature at the cell inlet
[kg/s] WFUM flue gas flow rate
[ ] FSFU dirty factor
[%] %PCO CO2 partial pressure
[%] %PHO H2O partial pressure
[%] %O2F oxygen percentage in the flue gas
[W] QSCA power transferred to the pipe metal
[K] TFUC flue gas temperature at the cell outlet

MODULE FUMB

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of module input-output variables (meanings defined above) user must assign the following data:

[ ] NTUB number of pipes (in parallel configuration) in the tube bundle
[m] DIAE external diameter of tube bundle pipes
[m] LUNG cell length
[m] PASP step of pipe bench perpendicular to the flue gas flow
[m] PASL step of pipe bench longitudinal to the flue gas flow
[m²] SCAN transverse cross section area of the flue gas duct
[m²] SENC enclosure exchanging arrangement
[ ] FA factor of geometrical arrangement
[ ] SIGN  agreement of exchanged power sign
(+ for EVAP and TUBS modules, - for TH20)

To represent the tube wall (only one row of pipes), assign: PASL < 0 and FA = 1.
