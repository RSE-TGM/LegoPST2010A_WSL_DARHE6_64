Heat transfer correlations for flue gas

The thermal power exchanged by flue gas along the boiler backpass is mainly yielded to the exchanger tubes by several banks of heaters. In consideration of their geometrical disposition, one can suppose that the heat transfer between water and flue gas is of crossflow type.

Still following a Babcock & Wilcox metodology also radiative heat transfer coefficient grad can be taken into account.

It can be expressed as a product of two terms, the first one depending on the temperature difference between walls and gas and the wall temperature, the second one depending on the partial pressure of triatomic gases (only CO2 and H2O are considered) and the radiating thickness, which on its turn depends on the tubes arrangement. The two terms are reported as charts and are not shown here.
