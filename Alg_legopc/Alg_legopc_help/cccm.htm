MODULE CCCM

CARD A

Model Generalities

BOILER FURNACE 1-D MODEL (POLY-FUEL)

 

The CCCM module simulates the functioning of a boiler combustion furnace fed, also simultaneously, by different fuels (coal, natural gas, fuel oil).

It particularly simulates the thermodynamic behaviour of fluid present on the "air/flue gas side" of combustion furnace. In order to realize a complete combustion furnace model, user must interconnect the CCCM and EVAP modules. EVAP simulates the thermo-hydraulic behaviour of water flowing inside the tube bundles forming the combustion furnace walls and the influence that the metal of pipes has in the thermal exchanges between flue gas and water.

The physical-mathematical model, encoded in the CCCM, is referred to a boiler combustion furnace equipped with or without "NOx-ports". The combustion furnace is subdivided in "NCEL" axial cells (3<NCEL<10) (see Fig.1). In every cell the flue gas exchanges heat with "NPi" surfaces having different temperatures (max. 9 per cell and altogether max. 48) (see note 1).

Combustion stoichiometric balance in each cell is based on the fuel and air quantity actually present inside the cell taking into account the following flow rates:

- introduced from outside

- made available from other cell burners training

- unburnt coming from the preceding cell

For the NBRUC cells with burner(1 < NBRUC < 6) (see note 2) the presence of several burners at the same cell is foreseen. They are represented in each of NBRUC cell as only one equivalent burner.

Further hypotheses introduced in developing the physic - mathematical model, encoded in this module, are the following:

- the monodimensional approximation with concentrated parameters is valid for the conservation equations of fluid mass and energy inside the combustion furnace

- links between the input and output quantities from control volumes (coinciding with the cell volumes), for which the conservation equations are written are instantaneous (static model). In other words, in the conservation equations the accumulation terms are not considered, so equations themselves are algebraical and not differential.

- To deal with the energetic exchanges the following thermal exchange mechanisms are considered:

a) the convective thermal exchange between the fluid flowing through the cell and the NPi cell exchange surfaces

b) the radiative thermal exchange between the fluid flowing through the cell and the NPi cell exchange surfaces

c) the radiative thermal exchange between the fluid flowing through the cell and the fluid flowing through the preceding and following cells.

fig. 1. Combustion furnace scheme

 

- the flue gas is assumed as a grey body (emissivity independent from radiation wavelength)

- the combustion furnace walls are assumed as a grey body with constant emissivity (independent, particularly, from temperature)

- the combustion process is represented as a thermal power release corresponding to the air-fuel complete reaction of the fuel flow rate flowing into the cell(source terms).

- in the energetical balance related to one cell the source terms is only given by the whole thermal power coming from combustion inside the cell.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of fluid mass

2) the conservation equation of fluid energy

3) the equation of radiative thermal exchange between two fluids

4) the equation of radiative thermal exchange between one fluid and one surface

5) the equation of convective thermal exchange between one fluid and one surface

6) the partial pressure calculation equation for oxygen (O2) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

7) the partial pressure calculation equation for carbon dioxide (CO2) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

8) the partial pressure calculation equation for water (H2O) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

9) the fraction calculation equation for water (H2O) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet.

Further analyses related to physic-mathematical model development are available in the section concerning this module of the manual "LEGOCAD" - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants-Theory".

Note 1

Because of software limits regarding the solution of the whole equation system (maximum number of equations for each module) it needs that exchanging surfaces do not exceed 48, even if each cell can exchange heat with 9 surfaces having different temperatures.

Note 2

If the combustion furnace to simulate is equipped with NOx-ports, the cells to which the NOx-ports are associated must be considered as equipped with burners. User must assign to these cells zero fuel flow rate.

MODULE CCCM

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

NCEL = cells total number

NPi = number of exchanging surfaces in the i-th cell

NBRUC = number of cells equipped with burners

i = 1, 2, ... NCEL

j = 1, 2, ... NPi

k = 1, 2, ... NBRUC

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[K]  TF_i flue gas temperature inside the i-th cell
[W/m²/K] GCi equivalent total thermal exchange coefficient between flue gas and the i-th cell surfaces
[kg/s] WFCC total flow rate of gas flowing out from the combustion furnace
[%]  %O2 partial pressure (*) of oxygen present in flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet
[%] %CO2  partial pressure (*) of carbon dioxide present in flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet
[%] %H2O partial pressure (*) of water present in flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

(*) partial pressures are expressed in percent of flue gas pressure: in fact they are molar (and volumetric) fractions (multiplied by 100)
[%]  %FMA mass fraction of water present in flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet in percent
[K]  TMij 

temperature of the j-th exchanging surface inside the i-th cell
[kg/s] WCOk fuel flow rate to the k-th burner
[ ]  ICOk

fuel type to the k-th burner (1. for gas, 2. for fuel oil, 3. for coal)
[kg/s]  WARk flow rate of comburent (air and recirculating flue gas) to the k-th burner
[deg] ABRk training arc of the k-th burner
[kg/s] WRIF flow rate of recirculating flue gas supplied into the combustion furnace from hopper
[K] TRIF temperature of recirculating flue gas supplied into the combustion furnace from hopper
[kg/s] WARC  total flow rate of comburent air supplied into the combustion furnace (recirculating gases excluded, see note 3)
[K] TACO temperature of combustion flue gas supplied into the combustion furnace from burners
[ ] FACC convective thermal exchange corrective factor (see note 4)
[ ] FACR radiative thermal exchange corrective factor (see note 4)
[ ] AKFL flame length corrective factor (see note 5)
[kg/s]  WGAT total flow rate of tempering gas supplied into the combustion furnace (see note 6)

 

Note 3

Air total flow rate is understood for all burners, included NOx - port; for all burners, air is homogeneously blended with recirculation flue gas.

Note 4

FACC,FACR are corrective factors inserted in the total thermal exchange calculation for changing its entity.

This may be useful in case user has to determine some stationary conditions where quantities like the exchanged power or the flue gas temperature into given cells are known.

Note 5

The AKFL factor allows the setting of proportionality constant to calculate the fictitious flame length from the injected fuel flow rate; this calculation gives one of the elements to evaluate training effects.

Note 6

The WGAT variable (total flow rate of tempering gas flowing into the combustion furnace) represents a module optional input. During topology building, it is asked to user if in the combustion furnace model a tempering gas contribution is foreseen.

If the answer is affirmative the WGAT flow rate appears among the module variables and included among the input variables, otherwise the WGAT flow rate is not considered.

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

[ ]

NCEL (max.10.) cells total number
[ ] NBRUC (max. 6.) number of cells equipped with burners
[ ] 

EMISPAR combustion furnace walls emissivity
[ ]

ITMPRING parameter indicating if tempering gas is flowing (or not) into the combustion furnace

for ITMPRING > 0 there is gas tempering into the combustion furnace, at the indicated cell

for ITMPRING < 0 (or = 0) there is not tempering gas flowing into the combustion furnace
[ ] UMIDARIA mass fraction of water present in the comburent air (air humidity)
[ ] IBRUCk with k = 1, 6 relation index between cells and burners (see note 6)
[kg/s] WNCOGAS, WNCONAFT, WNCOCARB nominal flow rates per burner for each of the three fuel

for gas:

[ ]

CARBGAS, HYDRGAS, SOLFGAS, NITRGAS, OXYGGAS, UMIDGAS except UMIDGAS, which is the fraction of water mass present in the fuel, other data represent the mass fraction of each component in remaining dry gas
[1/ft/K]  ELUMGAS luminous contribution to the gas emissivity
[J/kg] ENTGAS gas reference enthalpy
[J/kg] PCIGAS net heat value

for fuel oil:

[ ] CARBNAFT, HYDRNAFT, SOLFNAFT, NITRNAFT, OXYGNAFT, UMIDNAFT except UMIDNAFT which is the fraction of water mass present in the fuel, other data represent the mass fraction of each component in remaining dry fuel;
[J/kg] ENTNAFT fuel oil reference enthalpy
[J/kg]  PCINAFT net heat value
[ ] SOOTCi i = 1, 10 volumetric fraction of solid particles (for fuel oil)

for coal:

[s] COMBTIME total combustion time
[kg/m³] ROCARB coal density
[m] DIAMPART particles mean diameter
[ ] ASHCARB mass fraction of ash on the dry
[J/kg] 

ENTCARB reference enthalpy
[J/kg] PCICARB net heat value
[ ] CARBCARB, HYDRCARB, SOLFCARB, NITRCARB, OXYGCARB, UMIDCARB except UMIDCARB, which is the fraction of water mass present in the fuel, a part of the dry fuel is composed by ash (solid substances not participating to the combustion); the ash fraction included in dry fuel is indicated by ASHCARB. Data listed before UMIDCARD represent the fractions in mass of elements present in the remaining substance.

In the module are available default values regarding the kind of fuels, so user may omit the input of unknown fuel characteristics and values.

Characteristic data about the cell geometry are the following:

data concerning each cell:

[m] LUNG (i) cell length
[m²] SEZ (i) cross section area of cell outlet
[m²] SUP(j) C (i) j-th exchange surface area of the i_cell
[ ] FCOjCi ratio between the exchange surface and his plain projection if <1, vice versa if >1

If several consecutive cells have the same data, it is enough to assign those concerning the first cell. The preceding cell data are automatically assigned to the following cells.

Even if in filling mask always appear the maximum number of data, user may fill the file F14 up to:

i = 1, 2, ... NCEL

j = 1, 2, ... NPi

k = 1, 2, ... NBRUC

Note 7 gives an example of this aspect.

Note 7

The simulation foresees a maximum of 6 cells equipped with burners. In the IBRUK_(k) fields user specifies, in a progressive order, the number of cell equipped with burners. If this number is lower than the maximum allowed, the remaining fields must be empty. E.g. if the cells total number is 6 and the cells 2 and 3 are only equipped with burners, user must indicate:

IBRUC_1 =2., IBRUC_2 =3., IBRUC_(k=3,4,5,6) =0.

It is an error to indicate: IBRUC_1 =3., IBRUC_2 =2.

 

MODULE CCCM

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

The fuel index can not decrease advancing with burners number, e.g. if in the k-th burner fuel oil is burning (ICOk = 2), in none of the following burners, having plane index from k+1 to NBRUC, gas can burn (ICOi = 1).

Corrective factor for the FACS thermal exchange (see note 3), must usually assume unitary value and NOTO must be imposed.

It can be free if user has to determine stationary conditions where the flue gas temperatures in some cells are known.

For the cells equipped with burners, user must indicate the air total flow rate flowing into the cell, and the fuel total flow rate related to the lighted burners present in the cell.

For the remaining variables no particular prescription is required.

MODULE CCCM

CARD E 

User's Subprograms

The CCCM module does not foresee user subprograms. For the air and flue gas enthalpy calculation, user must refer to the HTFU library routine, which gives the enthalpy on the basis of temperature and water mass fraction, apart from the fact that air, flue gas or their blending are used.
