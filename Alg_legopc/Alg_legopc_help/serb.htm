MODULE SERB

CARD A

Model Generalities

WATER RESERVOIR

 

The module SERB simulates the behavior of a water reservoir.

Environment pressure is imposed (input variable) and bottom pressure is calculated taking into account a gravitational term.

Evaporation is considered too when the enthalpy of the entering liquid is greater than the saturation enthalpy. In this case an evaporation time constant (fixed inside the module code as 10 s) is considered.

The level is kept in any case under a overflow value defined as a datum.

The three balance equations are used for the calculations:

  1. MASS CONSERVATION – to calculate the level of the reservoir

  2. MOMENTUM CONSERVATION – to calculate bottom pressure

  3. ENERGY EQUATION – to calcuate average enthalpy in the reservoir

The module SERB can be easily coupled with COEX . They simulate respectively the water and the steam part of a biphase cavity: COEX provides SERB with actual environment pressure and a condensation mass flow rate. SERB, in its turn, calculates an evaporation mass flow rate to be sent to the COEX.

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[m] LIVS water level
[Pa] PFSE pressure on bottom

[kg/s] WING input mass flow rate
[J/kg] HING input enthalpy
[kg/s] WOUT output mass flow rate

MODULE SERB

CARD F 

Residuals

Five residuals are written:

  1. Mass conservation (level calcualation): ODE seen by LEGO as algebraic equation

  2. Momentum conservation: algebraic equation calculating bottom pressure

  3. Energy conservation (enthalpy calculation): ODE seen by LEGO as algebraic

  4. Evaporation mass flow rate calculation: algebraic equation

  5. Evaporation enthalpy: algebraic equation
