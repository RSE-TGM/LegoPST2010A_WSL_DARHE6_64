MODULE RSCD

CARD A

Model Generalities

VERTICAL WATER PREHEATER

 

The RSCD module simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena which a fluid present in the cavity of a surface heater is subjected to. The simulation includes the thermal exchanges between the fluid in the cavity and the cooling fluid flowing through a tube bundle positioned inside the same cavity.

To define the mathematical model the RSCD module is referred to a vertical heater as schematized in figure 1. It consists of one cylindrical cavity having vertical axis containing a U-bend tube bundle with feed-water inlet and outlet on the same side. The cavity is subdivided in the following zones:

A) desuperheating zone, where the superheated steam, flowing into the heater, exchanges heat in countercurrent with the liquid flowing through the tube bundle up to become saturated steam

B) condensation zone, where the saturated steam condenses in consequence of the thermal exchange with the tube bundle, giving rise to a liquid flow rate feeding a subcooled zone

C) subcooled zone, where the liquid on cavity side exchanges heat in countercurrent with the liquid flowing through the tube bundle.

 

fig. 1.

To deal with the fluid on the cavity side the following hypotheses are considered:

- the thermal exchange with outside is negligible

- the energetical and mass exchanges (caused by surface phenomena at the interface between the condensing and subcooled zones) are negligible

- the thermal exchange surface between the fluid in the cavity and the tube bundle metal is constant in desuperheating zone. In the other zones it depends on the level of liquid present in the cavity

- the distribution of pressure in the cavity is uniform

- the distribution of enthalpy in each of the three zones (A, B, C) is uniform

- the density variations in subcooled zone (C) are negligible.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of mass in desuperheating zone

2) the conservation equation of energy in desuperheating zone

3) the conservation equation of mass in condensing zone

4) the conservation equation of energy in condensing zone

5) the conservation equation of mass in subcooled zone

6) the conservation equation of energy in subcooled zone.

In the equations 1), 2), and 4) the accumulations terms are not considered, so, in spite of the other ones, they are algebraical and not differential.

To deal with the tube bundle and fluid flowing through it, the following hypotheses are considered:

- through the tube bundle flows water at liquid state and under subcooled conditions

- the fluid pressure in the tube bundle is constant and equal to the inlet pressure

- the physical properties of tube bundle metal are constant

- the tube bundle pipes have thin thickness, so a discretization with only one radial cell is enough

- the heat longitudinal conduction is negligible both in metal and fluid flowing through the tube bundle.

The related equations are:

7) the energy conservation equation of fluid flowing through the tube bundle, applied to the desuperheating zone

8) the energy conservation equation, in the tube bundle metal, applied to the desuperheating zone

9) the energy conservation equation of fluid flowing through the tube bundle, applied to the condensation zone

10) the energy conservation equation in tube bundle metal, applied to the condensation zone

11) the energy conservation equation of fluid flowing through the tube bundle, applied to the subcooled zone

12) the energy conservation equation in the tube bundle metal, applied to the subcooled zone

13) the conservation equation of momentum about the fluid flowing through the tube bundle.

MODULE RSCD

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

Looking for the stationary condition, the initial value of the TMMD, HALC, TMMC, HALF and TMMF variables is calculated inside the module by using the tentative values assigned to the remaining variables. So their initialization is not needed.

To initialize remaining variables no particular prescription is required.

MODULE RSCD

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the module mathematical model (mentioned above) give rise to a set of residuals. Here there are only indicated their correspondences with the equations, also mentioning the respective normalization factors. Further explanations are given in the manual section: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".

RNI(1) - normalization factor 1.

it is originated by the conservation equation of mass in the subcooled zone.

RNI(2) - normalization factor P0

it is originated by the conservation equation of mass in the whole cavity.

RNI(3) - normalization factor H0

it is originated by the conservation equation of energy in the subcooled zone.

RNI(4) - normalization factor H0

it is originated by the conservation equation of energy in fluid flowing through the tube bundle applied at the desuperheating zone.

RNI(5) - normalization factor T0

it is originated by the conservation equation of energy in the tube bundle metal applied at the desuperheating zone.

RNI(6) - normalization factor H0

it is originated by the conservation equation of fluid energy flowing through the tube bundle applied at the condensation zone.

RNI(7) - normalization factor T0

it is originated by the conservation equation of energy in the tube bundle metal applied at the condensation zone.

RNI(8) - normalization factor H0

it is originated by the conservation equation of fluid energy flowing through the tube bundle applied at the subcooled zone.

RNI(9) - normalization factor T0

it is originated by the conservation equation of energy in the tube bundle metal applied at the subcooled zone.

RNI(10) - normalization factor P0

it is originated by the conservation equation of momentum in the fluid flowing through the tube bundle.
