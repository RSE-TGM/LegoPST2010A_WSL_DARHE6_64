MODULE RINA

CARD A

Model Generalities

HEAVY OIL HEATER

 

The RINA module simulates the operation of a steam heater for heavy oil.

The heater is of the "shell and tube" type; steam condensates inside the tube bundle, while heavy oil flows through the bundle and the shell.

All the pipes of the tube bundle have been represented with a unique equivalent tube.

The heavy oil path has been represented in diagram with n cells (maximum 5).

Main hypothesis introduced in the model are:

* Heavy oil reaching the heater, which must be all of the same type, perfectly mixes inside each cell;

* Heavy oil has a very low compressibility, which depends neither on temperature nor on pressure;

* The steam perfectly mixes inside the volume occupied by the same;

* The specific heat and the thermal conductivity of the pipe and heater wall material are constant;

* Thermal exchange coefficient between steam and pipes, between heavy oil and wall and between wall and external environment are constant.

 

The equations forming the mathematical model are

for each cell:

- Conservation equation of heavy oil mass;

- Conservation equation of heavy oil energy;

- Conservation equation of pipe wall energy;

- Equation of thermal exchange between heavy oil and the pipe wall;

- Equation of thermal exchange between heavy oil and the heater wall;

 

for the heater inside:

- Conservation equation of heavy oil mass;

- Conservation equation of the steam mass;

- Conservation equation of steam energy;

- Conservation equation of the energy of the heater wall;

- Equation of thermal exchange between steam and the pipe wall;

- Equation of thermal exchange between the heater wall and the otuside environment.

 

The type of heavy oil considered is determined through an appropriate index assigned among input data.

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

N = number of cells of the tube bundle schematization (max. 5)

i = 1, 2, ..., N

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF THE MODULE INPUT - OUTPUT VARIABLES

[K] TNUi temperature of heavy oil coming out from the i-th cell
[K] TPFi temperature of the wall of the i-th cell
[kg/s] WNUi flow rate of heavy oil coming out from the i-th cell
[J/kg] HVUC enthalpy of steam coming out from the heater
[Pa] PVTU steam pressure
[Pa] PNRI heavy oil pressure in the heater
[K] TMRI temperature of the heater wall
[kg/s] WNIN flow rate of heavy oil entering the heater
[K] TNIN temperature of heavy oil entering the heater
[kg/s] WNUR flow rate of heavy oil coming out from heater
[kg/s] WVIN flow rate of steam entering the heater
[J/kg] HVIN enthalpy of steam entering the heater
[kg/s] WVUS flow rate of steam coming out from heater
[K] TEST temperature of environment outside the heater
[W/m²/K] CSES thermal exchange coefficient between the heater wall and the outside environment

 

Note 1 : In the model topology definition phase, the user shall be required to indicate the number of cells for the heater he intends to simulate.

In particular the following string shall appear on the screen:

NUMBER OF CELLS (MAX = 5) ?

 

Whenever the cell number is assigned, it is assumed equal to 1; if a number of cells > 5 is assigned the following string appears:

NUMBER OF CELLS N OUT OF LIMITS

 

and entering of the cell number is newly required.

The information relevant to the number of cells must be included also in the model geometrical data (see Card C).

MODULE RINA

CARD F 

Residuals

 

Residuals for the calculation of the steady state condition have the following expression:

for each one of the n cells (i = 1, ..., n)

IR = 4*(I-1)
RNI(IR+1) = WNIC*(HNIC-HNUC)+QNA(I)-QMA(I)

(norm. fact.W0*H0)
RNI(IR+2) = WNIC-WNUC

(norm fact. W0)
RNI(IR+3) = QVA(I)-QNA(I)

(norm fact. W0*H0)

for the whole heater

RNI(IR+4) = QVAP-(HVIN-HVUC)*WVIN

(norm fact. W0*H0)
RNI(IR+5) = WVIN-WVUS

(norm. fact. W0)
RNI(IR+6) = WNUC-WNUR

(norm. fact. W0)
RNI(IR+7) = QMAN-QAMB

(norm. fact.W0*H0)

where:

WNIC flow rate of heavy oil entering the i-th cell
WNUC flow rate of heavy oil coming out from the i-th cell
HNIC enthalpy of heavy oil entering the i-th cell
HNUC enthalpy of heavy oil coming out from the i-th
QNA(I) power exchanged by heavy oil with pipes of the i-th cell
QMA(I) power exchanged by heavy oil with the shell of the i-th cell
QVA(I) power exchanged by steam with pipes of the i-th cell
QVAP total power exchanged by steam with pipes
QMAN total power exchanged by heavy oil with the shell
QAMB total power exchanged by the shell with the outside environment
WVIN steam flow rate entering the heater
WVUS steam flow rate coming out from heater
HVIN enthalpy of steam entering the heater
HVUC enthalpy of steam coming out from heater
WNUC heavy oil flow rate coming out from the i-th cell
WNUR heavy oil flow rate coming out from heater

The expression of residuals for the calculation of the transient condition has not been indicated. We notice that in differential equations, time derivatives are expressed as finished differences, so that the algebraic-differential equation system has been transformed into a system of algebraic equations.
