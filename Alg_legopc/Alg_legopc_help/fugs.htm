MODULE FUGS

CARD A

Model Generalities

CONVERTION OF FLUE GASES CHEMICAL COMPOSITION

 

The modules of the standard thermodynamical library of the LEGOCAD System related to the simulation of flue gas ducts of the thermal power plants have to read the chemical composition of the flue gas in a particular way.

So it is necessary that the chemical composition of the flue gas is converted from that supplied by the combustion chamber as mass fractions of each chemical component; at the same time it must be taken into account the dilution of the flue gas caused by the turbine air injections and the blow-off (when these valves are opened).

Finally the total flue gas flow rate is evaluated again and low-bounded with an user assignable value in order to don't supply to the boiler a null flow rate of flue gas when the machine is halted (always for demands of the library modules related to the simulation of the flue gas ducts).

The module that performs these conversions is called FUGS.

The chemical composition of the flue gas at the combustion chamber outlet is defined by the mass fractions of CO2, H2O, O2 and Ar (the mass fraction of N2 is given by the complement to one). Both for the air injected in the turbine cavities and for that which comes from the blow-off valves it is used the chemical composition of the international standard dry air, corrected adding the opportune fractions of aqueous steam and carbonic anhydrite.

MODULE FUGS

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

NFLOWS = number of additive air flows

i = 1, NFLOWS

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WFUM Flue gas flow rate before the dilution
[ ] FCO2 Mass fraction of CO2 in the flue gas before the dilution
[ ] FH2O Mass fraction of H2O in the flue gas before the dilution
[ ] FFO2 Mass fraction of O2 in the flue gas before the dilution
[ ] FFAR  Mass fraction of Ar in the flue gas before the dilution
[kg/s] WARi Air flow rate of the i-th additive flow
[kg/s] WTOT Total flue gas flow rate at the boiler inlet (kg/s)
[%] ZCO2 Molar fraction of CO2 in the flue gas in percentage
[%] ZPH2 Molar fraction of H2O in the flue gas in percentage
[%] ZPO2 Molar fraction of O2 in the flue gas in percentage
[%] ZH2O Mass fraction of H2O in the flue gas in percentage

 

INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES NORMALIZATION FACTORS

Variables Normalization
WFUM, WARi, WTOT W0
FCO2, FH2O, FFO2, FFAR 0.1
ZCO2, ZPH2, ZPO2, ZH2O 1.

MODULE FUGS

CARD F 

Residuals

 

RNI(1) = WTOT - WFUM - WARI

 

IF (KREGIM) THEN

    RNI(2) = (ZCO2 - ZCO2C) / 10.

    RNI(3) = (ZPH2 - ZPH2C) / 10.    

    RNI(4) = (ZPO2 - ZPO2C) / 10.

    RNI(5) = (ZH2O - ZH2OC) / 10.

ELSE

    RNI(2) = ZCO2C

    RNI(3) = ZPH2C

    RNI(4) = ZPO2C

    RNI(5) = ZH2OC

ENDIF

Note: during transient simulation, the outputs related to the chemical composition of the flue gas are not-iterative outputs and the Jacobian matrix (related to the one iterative output) is evaluated algorithmically; during the steady state evaluation all the five outputs are iterative, and the Jacobian matrix is evaluated numerically calling the NAJAC subroutine.
