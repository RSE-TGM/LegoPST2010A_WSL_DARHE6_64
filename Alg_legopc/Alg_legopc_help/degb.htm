MODULE DEGB

CARD A

Model Generalities

DEGASSING UNIT

 

The DEGB module simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena to which the liquid-steam mix contained in the cavity of a counterflow degassing unit of the "SPRAY-TRAY" type is subjected. This type of degassing unit consists of a large horizontal cylindrical tank, acting as collection basin of the degassed liquid, with a second horizontal cylindrical element (deaerator reservoir) where gas elimination from the liquid takes place.

The operation principle is the following: the condensate to be degassed is sprayed in the upper part of the deaerator reservoir and falls on a set of "U" section bars, generally known as "basins".

When basins are full, the liquid in excess overflows and is collected by a second set of basins, placed under the first one and shifted with respect to the same. The liquid in excess newly overflows from there towards a further set of basins until it reaches the deaerator reservoir bottom. During the descent from the deaerator reservoir top to down, the liquid exchanges heat in countercurrent with the spilling steam let into the lower part of the deaerator reservoir.

The liquid collected on the deaerator reservoir bottom, falls through two appropriate pipes, in the collecting tank underneath. The deaerator reservoir and the collecting tank are also connected between them through two additional pipes, called "venting tubes" whose function is that to equalise the pressure in the two vessels in any operation condition.

To obtain the mathematical model simulating the degassing unit as a whole the deaerator reservoir and the tank have been considered as two separate cavities. A different mathematical model has been adopted for each cavity, and the two different models have been put in relation between them through an additional model simulating the function of venting tubes.

This allows a more realistic determination of pressure, to adjustment purposes and the simulation of phenomena such as steam recall from tank to the deaerator reservoir in case of violent depressurization.

The mathematical model used to simulate the deaerator reservoir is essentially that of a cavity in which the phases (liquid, steam) are in equilibrium. Main hypothesis assumed are the following:

- absence of non condensable gas.

- phases of the liquid-steam mix in thermodynamic equilibrium between them also during a transient state;

- uniform distribution of pressure and enthalpy;

- thermal equilibrium between the fluid and the basins steel;

- adiabatic cavity walls;

- thermal exchange between steam and basin steel neglectable;

- all the liquid present in the cavity in excess with respect to the basins capacity, overflows and reaches the collection tank.

Equations used, both differential, are:

  1. mass conservation equation in the deaerator reservoir;

  2. energy conservation equation in the deaerator reservoir.

The mathematical model relevant to venting tubes essentially consists of a unique algebraic equation putting the steam flow rate flowing through the pipes in relation with the pressure difference between tank and deaerator reservoir. This equation is obtained applying the conservation equation of motion quantity to venting tubes.

The mathematical model used for the tank, contrarily to what has been made for the deaerator reservoir, is that of a cavity in which the two phases, liquid and steam, can be in non thermodynamic equilibrium between them. In particular the tank volume is divided into two areas having variable limits: an upper area identified as "steam area", and a lower area identified as "liquid area". In both the areas, which can be in non thermodynamic equilibrium between them, water can be present as biphase mix. This enables to consider the condensation rate proportional to the mass of liquid present in the steam area, and the evaporation rate proportional to the steam mass present in the liquid area.

This model allows to simulate liquid expansion phenomena and the flashing deriving from a quick depressurization.

Further hypothesis included in the model are:

- uniform distribution of pressure and enthalpy in the steam area;

- uniform distribution of enthalpy in the liquid area;

- linear pressure distribution, with respect to height in the liquid area;

- thermal exchange between fluid and cavity walls neglactable.

 

Equations used are:

3) conservation equation of mass in the steam area;

4) conservation equation of energy in the steam area ;

5) conservation equation of mass in the liquid area;

6) conservation equation of mass in the liquid area;

7) equation for liquid level calculation in the tank;

8) equation for the calculation of pressure on the cavity bottom.

 

The first four, out of these ones are differential equations while the remaining ones are algebraic.

The equation 8) useful to the purposes of a correct evaluation of NPSH of the liquid extraction pump, has been formulated for an horizontal axis, cilindrycal cavity. Characteristic dimensions of this cavity must be supplied in data required by the input module.

Finally it must be noticed that it is possible to simulate both the heating phase of the degassing unit during starting, and the pump recycle.

MODULE DEGB

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables of the DEGB module, whose meaning has been defined in card B, the user must assign the following set of data to complete the input required by the module:

[m³] V.TORRET deaerator reservoir internal volume, available for the fluid (deaerator reservoir vol. - internal overall dimensions vol.)
[m³] V.BAC total capacity of basis (volume of liquid collected by basins in case the same are all full)
[J/K] CTM BAC thermal capacity of the basin steel
[m³] VOL.SERB tank internal volume, available for the fluid (tank vol. overall dimensions vol.)
If this datum is not assigned, it is calculated using the diameter "DIA.SERB." and the length "LUN.SERB"
[m] DIA.SERB tank internal diameter
[m] LUN.SERB tank internal length

MODULE DEGB

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

In the search of the steady condition, the initial value of average enthalpy HTOR is calculated inside the module using the tentative values assigned to the remaining variables. Theferore, initialization by the user is not required.

For the initialization of the remaining variables no special prescription is required.

Residuals

The equations forming the module mathematic model, indicated in cards A1 and A2, respectively, provide a set of residuals, which expression, both extended and formal would require a number of comments beyond the scope of this manual. We therefore prefer to indicate consistancies between residuals and equations, mentioning also the relevant normalization factors and make reference to the manual section, relevant to the module: "Model library of the LEGO code for the simulation of power generation plants - Theory"

for additional details.

RNI(1) - normalization factor DP0
originated by the conservation equation of motion quantity applied to balancing

RNI(2) - normalization factor W0*H0
relevant to the conservation equation of energy in the deaerator reservoir

RNI(3) - normalization factor W0
relevant to the mass conservation equation applied to the whole degassing unit

RNI(4) - normalization factor W0*H0
relevant to the conservation equation of energy in the steam area

RNI(5) - normalization factor W0*H0
relevant to the conservation equation of energy in the liquid area

RNI(6) - normalization factor W0
relevant to the conservation equation of mass in the liquid area

RNI(7) - normalization factor P0
relevant to the equation for the calculation of pressure in the deaerator reservoir

RNI(8) - normalization factor L0
represents an implicit equation for the calculation of the LIVD level

RNI(9) - normalization factor P0
relevant to the equation for the calculation of pressure on tank bottom

RNI(10) - normalization factor H0
relevant to assigning of enthalpy of the liquid extracted from the tank
