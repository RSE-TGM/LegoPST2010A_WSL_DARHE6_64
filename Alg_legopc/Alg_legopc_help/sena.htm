MODULE SENA

CARD A

Model Generalities

OIL RESERVOIR

 

The SENA module simulates the behaviour of a cavity with free upper surface, where heavy or lubrificating oil is introduced from one or more inlet ducts and exhausted by one or more other ducts. In this element the thermal energy accumulation is taken into account.

Main module basic hypothesis are:

* fluid inside the volume perfectly mixed, such to consider as uniform the outlet temperature.

 

Equations used are:

1) fluid energy conservation

2) fluid mass conservation

3) level equation, function of the constant section and of the volume of fluid

4) bottom pressure equation, function of fluid level and density

MODULE SENA

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

NE = number of incoming ducts

NU = number of outgoing ducts

i = 1, ..., NE

j = 1, ..., NU

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WINi mass flow rate from the i-th inlet duct
[K] TINi fluid temperature at the i-th inlet duct
[kg/s] WUSj mass flow rate to the j-th outlet duct
[Pa] PSUP free surface pressure
[K] TNAF fluid output temperature
[m³] VOLF volume of fluid in the tank
[m] LIVE fluid level
[Pa] PFON fluid pressure at the bottom

Physical and Geometrical Data

MODULE SENA

CARD F 

Residuals

 

Residuals have the following expression:

RNI(1) = (SWHI - SWI*HU)/(MASS*CP*T0)

RNI(2) = (SWI - SWU)/(RO*VOL0)

RNI(3) = (LIVE - VOL/SEZ)/LIV0

RNI(4) = (PFON - PSUP - RO*9.81*LIVE)/DP0

 

where:

HU oil enthalpy, determined by the subprogram for the calculation of oil properties
SWHI sum of entering enthalpic flow rates
SWI sum of entering mass flow rate
SWU sum of outlet mass flow rate
MASS oil mass in the tank
RO oil density
LIVE oil level in the tank
VOL oil volume in the tank
SEZ cross (horizontal) section of the tank
PFON bottom pressure
PSUP pressure at the free surface
