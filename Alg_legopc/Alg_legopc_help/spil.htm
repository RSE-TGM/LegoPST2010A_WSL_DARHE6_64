Model Generalities

HEATING STEAM BLEEDING

 

The SPIL module simulates an heated steam bleeding from a turbine stage for feedwater preheating.

The bleedings are drawn by the valves positioned between turbine and preheaters. Two of these are motor-operated valves and respectively located downstream turbine inlet and upstream preheater. A check valve is positioned between the two motor-operated valves.

The mathematical model consists of only one algebraical equation deriving from the energy conservation equation.

The main module hypoteses are:

- in thermal exchange between steam and feedwater, only the steam evaporation heat is considered

- the temperature to which water exchanges heat (used for the exchange coefficient calculation) is the weighted mean temperature of water:

Tm = 0.3 * Ti + 0.7 * Tu

It is hypotized that:

ys (Tsat-Tm) = Wsp (hvs - hls)

and that the exchange coefficient 'y' is proportional to Wal.

Then:

ys = y* Wal where y* is constant

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WSPI flow rate of steam bled from the turbine
[Pa] PTUR bleeding steam pressure
[kg/s] WALI flow rate of feedwater in the preheater
[J/kg] HALI feedwater enthalpy at the preheater inlet
[J/kg] HALU feedwater enthalpy at the preheater outlet
[Pa] PALI feedwater pressure in the preheater
[p.u.] ALZ1 lift of motor-operated valve on the bleeding
[p.u.] ALZC lift of check valve
[p.u.] ALZ2 lift of motor-operated valve at the preheater inlet

MODULE SPIL

CARD F 

Residuals

The module has only one residual whose expression is:

RNI(1) = WSPI - WSPC

where WSPC is the bleeding flow rate calculated as described above

If the product ALZ1*ALZ2 < 1.0E-4, the WSPC flow rate is considered zero.

Thus:

RNI(1) = WSPI
