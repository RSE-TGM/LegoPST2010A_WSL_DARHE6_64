MODULE SERA

CARD A

Model Generalities

COMPRESSED AIR TANK

 

The SERA module simulates a cavity where gas is let in from one or more ducts and exhausted to one or more other ducts. The number of inlet and outlet ducts is selected by the user. In this element, the mass accumulation phenomenon is prevailing.

Main module basic hypothesis are :

- non adiabatic cavity walls

- thermal accumulation inside cavity walls

- gas inside the volume perfectly mixed, such to consider its temperature as uniform.

- the perfect gas law applies to the fluid inside the cavity.

Equations used are:

1) gas energy conservation equation

2) gas mass conservation equation.

3) energy balance of cavity metallic wall

The module maintains its validity in case of flow inversion in the inlet and/or outlet ducts.

[kg/s] WU_j gas flow rate at ordinary outlet from tank to j-th duct
[K] TU_j gas ordinary outlet temperature with flow inversion
[K] TEST external temperature
[W/m²K] XCSE tank - environment thermal exchange coefficient
[Pa] PSER pressure in tank
[K] TFPL  tank metallic wall temperature
[K] TSER gas temperature in the tank

MODULE SERA

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of input and output variables of the SERA module, whose meaning has been defined in Card B, the user must assign the following data to complete the input required by the module:

[ ] N.TU.EN number of inlet ducts
[ ] N.TU.US number of outlet ducts
[m³] VolSer tank internal volume
[m] DiamInt tank internal diameter
[m] Spess tank wall thickness
[kg/m³] DensMet density of tank wall material
[J/kgK] CalSpMet specific heat of tank wall material
[W/m²K] CoefScIn thermal exchange coefficient between tank wall and fluid contained in the tank itself (the default value is 300.)
[ ] IGAS gas type: 1 for air, 2 for methane, 3 for hydrogen

MODULE SERA

CARD F 

Residuals

To show the expressions of the solving residuals is not very enlightening; for that, it should need other explications, that should ask to go back in the mathematical model, but it goes beyond the scope of this document: for the user, it will be enough take note that the three residuals originated from the mathematical model, as already mentioned in Card A1, represent:

- the time derivative of the gas pressure inside the tank;

- the time derivative of the tank metal wall temperature;

- the rime derivative of the gas temperature inside the tank.

The evaluation of the 1st and the 3rd derivatives is performed inside the SERA module solving (internally) an algebraic system (obtained from the manipulation of the gas mass and energy conservation equations).
