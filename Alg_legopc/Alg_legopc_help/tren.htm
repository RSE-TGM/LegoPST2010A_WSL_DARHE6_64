MODULE TREN

CARD A

Model Generalities

STEAM TURBINE REACTION STAGE

 

The TREN module simulates one or more reaction stages of a steam turbine merely from a thermodynamic point of view. The module main simplifying hypotheses are:

- steam accumulation phenomena in the turbine small volumes are not considered because to the steam are associated time constants much more smaller than those typical of thermohydraulic phenomena interesting in simulating the functioning of energy production energy

- thermal exchanges between steam and metal are not considered

- drainages event is excluded

- stage efficiency is only considered as a function of revolving speed and flow rate

The model allows the following stage simulations:

- under functioning normal conditions

- under start-up conditions

- under load reject and tripping conditions.

Generally it is advisable to assemble all stages included between two consecutive bleeding in only one equivalent stage.

The stage is simulated by the following algebraical equations:

1) calculation equation of end expansion enthalpy, obtained by using the thermodynamic efficiency definition. The efficiency is calculated utilizing conditions described in "physical and geometrical data" section.

2) balance equation between inlet and outlet stage flow rates as well as possible bleedings

3) equation which define the relation between inlet and outlet pressures as a function of flow rate (Stodola's equation) (see note 1)

4) calculation equation of mechanics power supplied by the stage.

In the TREZ module possible drainages are taken into account while in the TREN module no drainages are considered; this is the fundamental difference between them.

Drainage use is only significant in nuclear power turbines (saturated steam feeding) while in conventional power stations the condensate presence may only occur on low pressure turbine final stages. Anyway the error made do not taking into account these drainages is negligible. Consequently also in simulating of low pressure turbine final stages the TREN module can be utilized. On the contrary in nuclear turbine simulation the TREN module use is needed.

To simulate the whole turbine, besides the connection of several TREN modules, use of the STAZ module is required. It simulates the turbine control stage.

Bleeding present in reaction stages are represented by using the VALV or SPIL modules (see related documentation).

To simulate start-up or load rejection or turbine tripping, user has to utilize the MART module for revolving speed calculation and (see user documentation in STAZ  module) a model concerning the regulation chain of turbine revolving speed.

Note 1

The Stodola's law is expressed in this module as follows:

PITZ²-PUTZ² = KEFL*WITZ²*PITZ/ROI

where:

- KEFL is the flow coefficient calculated following the instructions described in "physical and geometrical data" section

- ROI is the density of fluid at the stage inlet.

Remaining symbols are mentioned below.

Note 2

In the module the ETAZ thermodynamic efficiency is calculated as:

ETAZ = ETANOM * (2*X - X²) * ETAW

where:

- ETANOM is the thermodynamic efficiency under nominal conditions calculated following the instructions described in "physical and geometrical data" section

- X is the ratio between actual and nominal speed (runs per minute)

- ETAW is a corrective factor only used for low flow rates as regards to PORT_IN nominal flow rate (see below).

By using experimental measuring carried out on turbines the following relation has been deduced:

ETAW = 4.969 * (WITZ/PORT__IN) + 0.18

applicable for the WITZ value lower than 0.165 PORT_IN. In other cases ETAW has a unitary value.

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WITZ fluid flow rate at the stage inlet
[kg/s] WSPZ bleeding flow rate

[ ] SERC load rejection or tripping signal

[J/kg] HUTZ fluid enthalpy at the stage outlet
[kg/s] WUTZ fluid flow rate at the stage outlet
[Pa] PITZ fluid pressure at the stage inlet
[W] QMEC mechanic power supplied by the stage.

MODULE TREN

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial value of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

[Pa] PRES__IN steam pressure at the stage inlet under nominal conditions
[J/kg] ENTA__IN steam enthalpy at the stage inlet under nominal conditions
[kg/s] PORT__IN steam flow rate at the stage inlet under nominal conditions
[Pa] PRES_OUT steam pressure at the stage outlet under nominal conditions
[J/kg] ENTA_OUT steam enthalpy at the stage outlet under nominal conditions
[rpm] RPMV_NOM turbine angular speed under nominal conditions.

The coefficient KEFL is calculated through the relation:

KEFL = (PRES__IN²-PRES_OUT²)*ROI0/(PRES__IN*PORT__IN²)

where ROIO is the fluid density at the stage inlet.

The ETANOM efficiency under nominal condition is obtained through:

ETANOM = (ENTA__IN - ENTA_OUT)/(ENTA__IN - HUIS0)

where HUISO is the outlet enthalpy which fluid would has if expansion in isentropic.

MODULE TREN

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the module mathematical model give rise, under normal functioning conditions, to the following residuals:

RNI(1) = HITZ-HUTZ-ETAZ*(HITZ-HUIS)

(normalization factor = H0)

RNI(2) = WUTZ+WSPZ-WITZ

(normalization factor = W0)

RNI(3) = PITZ*PITZ-PUTZ*PUTZ-KEFL*WITZ*|WITZ|*PITZ/ROI

(normalization factor = P0*P0)

RNI(4) = QMEC-WITZ*(HITZ-HUTZ)

(normalization factor = W0*H0).

The HUIS is the outlet enthalpy that fluid could has if its expansion is isentropic. Physical meanings of remaining variable are already defined in "topological data" section. The KEFL and ETAZ are described in first paragraph.

In case of start-up functioning conditions, the first residual has the following form:

RNI(1) = HITZ-HUTZ-ETAZ*(ENTA__IN - ENTA_OUT)

where ENTA_IN and ENTA_OUT quantities (see above) are the enthalpy values at the stage inlet and outlet under nominal functioning conditions.

The other residuals appear unchanged:

RNI(2) = WUTZ+WSPZ-WITZ

(normalization factor = W0)

RNI(3) = PITZ*PITZ-PUTZ*PUTZ-KEFL*WITZ*|WITZ|*PITZ/ROI

(normalization factor = P0*P0)

RNI(4) = QMEC-WITZ*(HITZ-HUTZ)

(normalization factor = W0*H0)

In case of load rejection or tripping under functioning conditions, the residuals equations are:

RNI(1) = HUTZ-HVS(PUTZ)

(normalization factor = H0)

RNI(2) = WUTZ+WSPZ-WITZ

(normalization factor = W0)

RNI(3) = PITZ-PUTZ-KEFL*WITZ*|WITZ|/ROI - KEFL*WITZ/10.0

(normalization factor = P0*P0)

RNI(4) = QMEC-WITZ*(ENTA__IN - ENTA_OUT)

(normalization factor = W0*H0).
