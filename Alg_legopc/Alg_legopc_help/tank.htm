MODULE TANK

CARD A

Model Generalities

STEAM-WATER TANK

 

The TANK module simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena in a cavity containing water in water and steam states tending to separate each other under force of gravity effect. Such module is commonly used to simulate plant components like:

- steam drums of boiler with natural or forced circulation

- "upper plenum" of PWR reactors steam generators

- "upper plenum" of BWR reactors

- drainage collecting and expansion tanks and flash tanks

- tanks usually containing water in liquid and steam phases

In the above-mentioned components, having a very similar purpose, a water-steam mixture flows in. The mixture is separated by means of gravity or centrifugal forces.

Steam and water conditions at the cavity outlet are imposed as identical to the nominal ones (total separation efficiency).

To modify the separation model it is necessary to intervene on FORTRAN's module code (see below).

In order to use the TANK module in building a circulation loop model (natural or forced), to define input and output variables the following items are taken into account:

- the outlet of liquid towards circulation loop

- the inlet of liquid-steam mixture coming from the circulation loop

- the inlet of subcooled feed-water.

In the mathematical model it is presumed that the entering subcooled liquid is uniformly mixed with all present in the cavity.

Further characteristics of the adopted mathematical model are:

- the liquid-steam phases may not be in thermodynamic equilibrium

- the condensation-evaporation phenomena between the liquid and steam phases are taken into account

- the thermal exchanges between internal fluid and cavity walls are not considered.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of mass in steam phase

2) the conservation equation of energy in steam phase

3) the conservation equation of energy in liquid phase

4) the conservation equation of mass in liquid phase

5) the equation for liquid level calculation as percentage of the measuring instruments working range

6) the equation for pressure calculation on the cavity bottom.

The first four equations are differential whereas the other ones are algebraical.

The equation 5), related to the level calculation, is formulated for a cavity having constant horizontal cross section area characterized by the data required in input (see below). If the cavity has not constant horizontal cross section area, user can assign again the equation 5) by means of a suitable subprogram (see below).

It is useful to note that TANK module is able to simulate a situation in which in the cavity only one of the two phases is present. So, it is possible to determine stationary conditions and transients in which the cavity is only filled by water or steam.

MODULE TANK

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

[ ] TYPE_CAV selection index about type of cavity to deal with (see below)
[m³] VOL.CAV. cavity internal volume (steam volume + water volume)
[ ] MISURA_Y selection index about the type of level measurement:
1. = the level is determined as elevation measure of the liquid surface, including the steam bubbles as inclusion in liquid phase;
-1. = the level is determined as elevation measure that liquid surface would have excluding the steam bubbles in liquid phase.
[%] X_OVER nominal ratio of steam flowing out from the cavity top (typical value 99. < X_OVER < 100.)
[%] X_UNDER nominal ratio of water flowing out from the cavity bottom (typical value X_UNDER = 0.0)
[m] ZERO_Y elevation gap between the liquid level corresponding to the zero of measuring instrument and the water outlet pipe
[m³] VOL_Y cavity volume between the zero of level measuring instrument and the water outlet pipe
[m] GAMMA_Y working range of the level measuring instrument
[m²] SEZ_Y horizontal cross section area of the cavity

To determine the liquid level, the TANK module refers to a cavity whose liquid volume below the measuring instrument nozzles is known and the level variation is proportional to the liquid volume variation. So it is a cavity where within the working range the horizontal cross section area is constant. If a collection basin having a more complex geometry is considered, user can provide the related data through the user's subprogram

SUBROUTINE TANKLIV(TYP, VLIV, Y, YTOT, AREA)

(see below). Knowing the engaged volume this subprogram calculates:

- the liquid level as a function of the cavity geometrical characteristics

- the proportional level of liquid as a function of the measuring instruments nozzles characteristic

- the area of separating surface between the liquid and steam phases.

In this case, user has to assign to the SEZ_Y a negative or zero value.

If this signal is found, the user subprogram is called and values of ZERO_Y, VOL_Y, GAMMA_Y, and SEZ_Y are ignored.

MODULE TANK

CARD E 

User's Subprograms

If the tank has a more complex geometry than the reference one, to provide the related data to the module, user has to assign a negative or zero value to SEZ_Y and write a user's subprogram whose declarative statement is:

SUBROUTINE TANKLIV(TYP, VLIV, Y, YTOT, AREA).

Knowing the engaged liquid volume such subprogram calculates:

- the level of liquid as a function of the cavity geometrical characteristics

- the proportional level of liquid as a function of the measuring instruments characteristics

- the area of separating surface between the liquid and steam phases

Input parameters: TYP, VLIV

Output parameters: Y, YTOT, AREA

[ ] TYP corresponds to the TYPE_CAV datum required in input (see above). It can be used as pointer to select the required cavity geometry in case user has to introduce different models of cavities into only one user subprogram
[m³] VLIV engaged liquid volume
[%] Y liquid level, as percentage of the measuring instruments working range
[m] YTOT level of liquid inside the cavity
[m²] AREA area of separating surface between liquid and steam phases.

Since cylindrical geometry having horizontal axis is one of the most usual geometries for drums, in the module the following subprogram is available:

SUBROUTINE TANKLIO(VLIV, RCAV, HLCAV, Y, YTOT, AREA),

which calculates (once the liquid volume contained inside an horizontal cylinder has been assigned) the level and area of contact surface between the liquid and steam phases.

The meanings of calling parameters are:

[m³] VLIV level of liquid inside the cylinder
[m] RCAV cylinder radius
[m] HLCAV cylinder length
[%] Y liquid level, as percentage of the measuring instruments working range
[m] YTOT level of liquid inside the cylinder
[m²] AREA area of separating surface between the liquid and steam phases.

Thereinafter it is indicated a TANKLIV subroutine example using the TANKLIO, related to a cavity consisting of a cylindrical drum having radius RCAV = 1 m and length HLCAV = 10 m, positioned on a parallelepiped having volume VOL_Y = 5 m³. The gap ZERO_Y between the zero of level measuring instrument and the pipe nozzle of flowing out liquid is 2 m. The level Y is indicated as percentage as regards to a range GAMMA_Y = 1.6 m centred round the horizontal axis of cylinder.

SUBROUTINE TANKLIV(TYP,VLIV,Y,YTOT,AREA)

IF(TYP.NE.1.) RETURN

GAMMA_Y=1.6

RCAV=1.

HLCAV=10.

VOL_Y=5.

ZERO_Y=2.

VCIL=VLIV-VOL_Y

CALL TANKLIO(VCIL,RCAV,HLCAV,Y,YTOT,AREA)

Y=(YTOT-(RCAV-GAMMA_Y/2.))/GAMMA_Y

YTOT=YTOT+ZERO_Y

RETURN

END

SUBROUTINE TANKLIO(VCAV,RCAV,HLCAV,Y,YTOT,SEZ)

C

C CALCULATES THE LEVEL IN A HORIZONTAL CYLINDER

C

C Y level divided 100

C YTOT level in meters

C SEZ area of liquid surface

C KNOWN:

C VCAV engaged volume

C RCAV cylinder radius

C HLCAV cylinder length

C

C EXECUTION

C

Y0=Y

VMAX=RCAV*RCAV*3.141592*HLCAV

IF(VCAV.LT.VMAX)GO TO 99

YTOT=2*RCAV

Y=YTOT/100.

SEZ=0.

RETURN

99 IF(VCAV.GT.1.E-3)GO TO 98

YTOT=0.

Y=0.

SEZ=0.

RETURN

C

C ITERATIVE CALCULATION ACCORDING TO NEWTON-RAPHSON'S

C

98 IT=0

Y=RCAV

1 IT=IT+1

C

C ALFA: ANGLE TO THE CENTRE OF CIRCULAR SEGMENT

C

ALFA=ACOS((RCAV-Y)/RCAV)

AST=RCAV**2.*ALFA-RCAV*(RCAV-Y)*SIN(ALFA)

C

C RESIDUAL

C

RES=VCAV-HLCAV*AST

C

IF(ABS(RES).LT.1.E-4)GO TO 10

IF(IT.GT.100)GO TO 20

C

C JACOBIAN

C

DALFA=1./(RCAV*SQRT(1.-((RCAV-Y)/RCAV)**2.))

DASOT=RCAV**2.*DALFA+RCAV*SIN(ALFA)+COS(ALFA)*RCAV*

$ DALFA*(Y-RCAV)

DVDY=DASOT*HLCAV

C

C ITERATION

C

DY=RES/DVDY

Y=Y+DY

GO TO 1

C

C SOLUTION

C

10 CONTINUE

YTOT=Y

SEZ=HLCAV*2*SQRT(2*RCAV*Y-Y*Y)

Y=Y/100.

RETURN

20 WRITE(6,1000)RES

$ 10X,'MAX NUM. ITERATION REACHED - RESIDUAL = ',E12.5//)

END

A "drying" phase (dehumidification) of steam may follow the mixture separation phase. This second phase is typical in steam generators of PWR and BWR nuclear reactors. The mathematical model of TANK module does not make distinction between the separation and drying phases. In addition, the outlet conditions of steam and liquid from the cavity are fixed by assigned input data and considered as boundary conditions.

In order to take into account the thermo-hydraulic conditions influence of mixture, at the cavity inlet, on separation-dehumidification process, user must intervene on the module programming, suitably modifying the subprogram:

SUBROUTINE TANKSEP(TYP, RXOV, RXUN, XR, WR, P, XOV, XUN).

Input parameters: TYP, RXOV, RXUN, XR, WR, P

Outlet parameters: XOV, XUN

[ ] TYP corresponds to the TYPE_CAV value required in input (see above). It can be used as pointer to associate to a given geometry of a selected cavity the efficiency of corresponding separation-dehumidification process, in case user has to introduce different models of this efficiency into only one user subprogram
[p.u.] RXOV nominal ratio of steam flowing out from the cavity (it corresponds to X_OVER datum assigned in input)
[p.u.] RXUN nominal ratio of water flowing out from the cavity (it corresponds to X_UNDER datum assigned in input)
[p.u.] XR enthalpy ratio of mixture at the cavity inlet
[kg/s] WR flow rate of mixture at the cavity inlet
[Pa] P pressure in the cavity
[p.u.] XOV ratio of steam flowing out from the cavity
[p.u.] XUN ratio of water flowing out from the cavity

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the module mathematical model give rise to the following residuals:

RNI(1) = WRG-WVCA+WEV-WCSUP-WC

(normalization factor = W0)

RNI(2) = WRG*(HRG-HVCA)+WEV*(HVSAT-HVCA)-WCSUP*(HVSAT-HVCA)+

-WC*(HWSAT-HVCA)-QSUP

(normalization factor = W0*H0)

RNI(3) = WAIC*(HAIC-HLCA)+WRL*(HRL-HLCA)+WCSUP*(HVSAT-HLCA)+

+WC*(HWSAT-HLCA)-WEV*(HVSAT-HLCA)+QSUP

(normalization factor = W0*H0)

RNI(4) = WAIC+WRL-WAUC+WCSUP+WC-WEV

(normalization factor = W0)

RNI(5) = LIV% - Y

(normalization factor = 100.)

RNI(6) = PFCA-PCAV-ROY*9.81*YTOT/P0

(normalization factor = P0)

Where:

- WRG flow rate related to the steam part of the mixture flowing into the cavity

- HRG its enthalpy

- WEV evaporation flow rate from the liquid zone

- WCSUP contribution to condensation flow rate produced by the surface phenomena at water-steam interface

- WC contribution to the condensation flow rate produced by total mass of steam present inside the cavity

- HVSAT enthalpy of saturated steam

- HWSAT enthalpy of saturated water

- QSUP exchanged thermal power between the liquid and steam through the separating surface

- WRL flow rate related to the liquid part of the mixture flowing into the cavity

- ROY water density.

The physical meanings of remaining variables are already defined above.

Further explanations about meanings of the above-mentioned quantities are available in the section concerning the TANK module of the manual: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".
