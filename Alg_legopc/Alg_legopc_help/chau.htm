MODULE CHAU

CARD A

Model Generalities

FEED WATER HEATER

The CHAU module simulates a feed water heater in steady state conditions. The simulation includes the thermal exchanges between the fluid in the cavity and the cooling fluid flowing through a tube bundle positioned inside the same cavity.

The mathematical model of CHAU module is referred to a heater represented in figure 1.

The cavity is subdivided in the following zones:

A) desuperheating zone, where the superheated steam, flowing into the heater, exchanges heat in countercurrent flow with respect to the feed water mass flow rate up to become saturated steam

B) condensation zone, where the saturated steam condenses in consequence of the thermal exchange with the tube bundle.

C) subcooled zone, where the liquid into the cavity exchanges heat in countercurrent flow with inlet heater mass flow rate.

fig. 1.

The mathematical model used equations are:

  1. the mass conservation equation of into the cavity

  2. the energy conservation equation

  3. the momentum conservation equation into the tube bundle

  4. the momentum conservation equation into the steam bleeding lines

  5. the steam pressure into the heater cavity considered in saturation conditions

  6. the steam temperature into the heater cavity considered in saturation conditions

  7. the temperature difference between the steam cavity and the outlet feed water flow.

  8. the temperature difference between the condense into the cavity and the inlet feed water flow.

MODULE CHAU

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial value of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:
[p] DPALN_NOM  friction losses in the tube bundle at nominal load
[Kg/s] WAL_NOM  feed water mass flow rate at nominal load
[p] DPVP_NOM  friction losses in the bleeding steam lines at nominal load
[Kg/s] WVP_NOM  steam mass flow rate at nominal load

MODULE CHAU

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

AKVP must be >0 and near the value 1.

DTSO must be 2<DTSO<10

DTSU is in general 0 or negative.

HCON must be less than the liquid phase saturation enthalpy value

RNI(3) = ((PVAP-PCAV) -

RNI(4) = (WVAP+WDRA-WCON)/W0

C--residuo n. 5 (equazione algebrica) *******

RNI(5) = (HCON-HEV(PCAV,STEV(PCAV,TCON,0.,1),1))/H0

RNI(7) = (WVAP*HVAP+WDRA*HDRA-WCON*HCON-WTUB*(HOUT-HINP))
