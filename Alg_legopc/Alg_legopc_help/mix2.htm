MODULE MIX2

CARD A

Model Generalities

WATER - STEAM ENTHALPIC MIXER

 

The MIX2 module simulates the perfect mixing of two fluids flowing together into a cavity.

Simplifying hypothesis adopted in the definition of the physical-mathematical model are the following:

- absence of thermal exchanges of the two fluids with the cavity walls;

- absence of mass and energy accumulation phenomena in the cavity;

Equations used, both algebraic ones, are:

1) the mass conservation equation;

2) the energy conservation equation.

The physical-mathematical model contained in the module cannot satisfactorily simulate mass flow inversion cases. If during the simulation of any transient state, the inversion of any mass flow rate should occur notwithstanding the simulation is not stopped, the trends of magnitudes obtained could be scarcely reliable. It is therefore necessary to check that during the transient state evolution this situation did not occur.

MODULE MIX2

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

The module can be interconnected with other modules according to two patterns:

1) mass flow rate as output variable of the first fluid entering the cavity
(prescription on block name: none)

 

 

2) mass flow rate as output variable of fluid coming out from cavity
(prescription on block name: preceded by '+')

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING ON THE MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[J/kg] HIM1 enthalpy of the first fluid entering the cavity
[J/kg] HIM2 enthalpy of the second fluid entering the cavity
[kg/s] WU12 mass flow rate of fluid coming out from the cavity
[kg/s] WIM2 mass flow rate of the second fluid entering the cavity
[kg/s] WIM1 mass flow rate of the first fluid entering the cavity
[J/kg] HU12 enthalpy of the fluid coming out from the cavity

MODULE MIX2

CARD F 

Residuals

Equations forming the module mathematical model, given in the relevant card A, provide the following residuals:

a) null mass flow rates and WIM1 output variable:
RNI (1) = - WIM1

(normalization factor = W0)
RNI (2) = 0.
b) null mass flow rates and WU12 output variable:
RNI (1) = WU12

(normalization factor = W0)
RNI (2) =0.
c) WIM1 < 0. and WIM2 > 0.
RNI (1) = WU12 - WIM1 - WIM2

(normalization factor = W0)
RNI (2) = HU12 - HIM2

(normalization factor = H0)
d) WIM2 < 0. and WIM1 > 0.
RNI (1) = WU12 - WIM1 - WIM2

(normalization factor = W0)
RNI (2) = HU12 - HIM1

(normalization factor = H0)
e) remaining cases:
RNI (1) = WU12 - WIM1 - WIM2

(normalization factor = W0)
RNI (2) = HU12*WU12 - HIM1*WIM1 - HIM2*WIM2

(normalization factor = W0*H0)
