MODULE VPNA

CARD A

Model Generalities

OIL PUMP

 

The VPNA module can simulate the operation of a pump in which the evolving fluid is heavy oil. This pump can be a centrifugal or a volumetric (i.e. positive displacement) one.

Main hypothesis at the model basis are:

- the fluid evolving in the pump is incompressible;

- in the pump vanes there is energy accumulation and heat loss across the walls is taken into account.

 

Equations forming the mathematical model are:

- characteristics curves of the component

- equation of energy conservation

- equation of power absorption

CARD B

Topological data

 

The module can be interconnected with other modules according to two patterns:

 

1) power absorbed as output variable (imposed speed)

prescription on block name: none

 

 

2) rotation speed as output variable (imposed power)

block name preceded by '+'

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[Pa] PIPN inlet pressure
[Pa] PUPN outlet pressure
[K] TNAF oil temperature at the outlet
[K] TUSC oil temperature at the inlet
[kg/s] WPNA fluid mass flow rate
[rad/s] OMPN rotation speed
[W] QPNA absorbed power
[K] TEXT external temperature

CARD F 

Residuals

The expressions of residuals varies according to calculation conditions (steady or transient state, type of pump, ecc.), therefore their description requires a treatment which goes beyond the scope of the user ‘s documentation. Anyway, it is sufficient to know that:

- the first residual concerns the performance of the pump; its form depends on the pump type (centrifugal or volumetric), but do not varies with the steady or transient state.

- the second residual concerns the equation of conservation of the energy; it takes into account also the heat loss, and its expression depends of steady or transient state: in the last case the residual contents the calculated derivative of oil temperature, that otherwise is zero.

- the third residual calculates the power absorption
