MODULE ALET

CARD A

Model Generalities

FINNED TUBE CELL

The module ALET belongs to the thermodinamic base library. It simulates the behaviour of a flue gas duct cell with finned exchange surface on the flue gas side.

The model not only evaluates the actual exchanging surface, but also it allows to simulate the dynamic behaviour of thermal exchange and storage in the fin metal.

The model of the fin also allows to simulate the (small) difference between the simulation of an exchanging plain pipe (with the same metal mass and the same exchanging surface) and of the finned pipe.

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[K] TMSU temperature of the surface of the pipe where the fin is connected

[kg/s] WFAL flue gas flow rate

[W] QTIL auxiliary state variable (no phisical meaning)
[W] QBNF filtered power given to the metal of the finned pipe

[W] QBNC power given to the metal of the finned pipe (not filtered)

[K] TFUC flue gas temperature at the cell outlet

MODULE ALET

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables (defined above), user must assign the following data:

[ ] NTUB number of parallel tubes in the pipe bundle

[m] DIAE external diameter of the pipe where the fin is connected

[m] LUNG lenght of the flue gas duct cell
[m] PASP pipe bundle perpendicular pitch (as regards to the flue gas direction)

[m] PASL pipe bundle longitudinal pitch (as regards to the flue gas direction)

[m²] SCAN tranversal section of the flue gas duct
[ ] FA geometric arrangement factor
[ ] SIGN convention for the exchanged power sign (positive sign for EVAF and TUBS; negative sign for TH2O)

[kg/m³] ROM metal density
[J/kg/ C] CM metal specific heat
[W/m/ C] COND metal thermal conductivity
[m] SPES fin thickness
[m] ALT fin height

[m] PASS finning helix pitch

[ ] NTBO number of pipes crossed by the flue gas in a transversal section

[1/m] N/ME it must be 0 if the fin is helical arrangement; otherwise it is the number of fins per meter (parallel fins)

Residuals

Normal flow rates:

C

RN(1)=(-TFF+TFM)/TAU/T0

C

RN(2)=(-QTILDE+AA*G*ALUNTOT*(TMET-TFF))/TAUN2/Q0

C

Q1=QTILDE+BB*G*ALUNTOT*(TMET-TFF)

RN(3)=(-Q1F+Q1)/TAU/Q0

DUEQ2=Q1F+2.*UTOT*ALAL*ALUNTOT*(TFF-TFM)

C

RN(4)=(SEGNO*QBANK-SBANK*UTOT*DELTF+Q1)/Q0

C

RN(5)=(WF*(HFI-HFU)-SBANK*UTOT*DELTF+DUEQ2)/Q0

 

Small flow rates:

RN(1)=(-TFF+TFM)/TAU/T0

RN(2)=(-QTILDE)/TAUN2/Q0

RN(3)=(-Q1F)/TAU/Q0

RN(4)=QBANK/Q0

RN(5)=(TFU-TMET)/T0
