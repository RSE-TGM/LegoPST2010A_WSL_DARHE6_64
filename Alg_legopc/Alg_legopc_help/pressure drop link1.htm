That very dependency may be written in many ways originating a great deal of correlations. Some of these are valid in laminar conditions. Other correlations are valid for turbulent motion, like Colburn’s, Drew’s, Blasius’s, Nikuradse’s or von Karman’s, which are valid in case of smooth tubes. If roughness becomes important, the ratio e/D, being e the average height of protuberances is introduced and two correlations (Nikuradse’s in the region of complete turbulence and Colebrook in the transition region) are currently used.
