MODULE RSOR

CARD A

Model Generalities

HORYZONTAL WATER PREHEATER

 

The RSOR module simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena which occur both inside the cavity and inside the tube bundle of a surface horizontal heater.

To define the mathematical model the RSOR module refers to a horizontal heater as schematized in figure 1. It consists of one cylindrical cavity having horizontal axis, containing a U-bend tube bundle with feed-water inlet and outlet on the same side. The cavity is subdivided in the following zones:

A) subcooled zone, where the tube bundle exchanges heat with a subcooled liquid

B) condensing zone, where the tube bundle exchanges heat only with steam.

In the "B" zone, steam condensing on the tube bundle's external surface, gives rise to a liquid flow rate collected on the cavity bottom going to feed the subcooled zone.

figure 1

In developing the mathematical model, it is assumed that, both in "A" and "B" zones the exchange surfaces between the fluid inside cavity and the tube bundle keep constant. So it is not taken into account that in consequence of a too high increase or decrease in liquid level inside the cavity can respectively occur the submersion of a part of the pipes which are in contact with steam or the emersion of a part of the submerged pipes which would be in contact with steam.

The cavity total volume is subdivided in two zones having variable boundaries respectively identified as condensing and subcooled zones.

In condensing zone, the fluid is dry saturated steam assuming that the liquid produced by condensation instantaneously separates, whereas in subcooled zone, the fluid is present as subcooled liquid. In condensing zone the accumulation term of energy conservation equation is not considered. Furthermore the assumed condensation flow rate is the one which in every moment satisfies the energetical balance of such zone.

To deal with the thermal exchanges between the fluid inside cavity and the tube bundle metal wall inside which feed-water is flowing, the tube bundle is assumed as subdivided in two cells respectively faced with "A" (subcooled) and "B" (condensing) zones.

Further simplifying hypotheses introduced in the cavity model are:

- the thermal exchange with cavity walls is negligible

- the distribution of pressure inside the cavity (pressure in condensing and subcooled zone are identical) is uniform

- the distribution of enthalpy in condensing zone is uniform

- the distribution of enthalpy in subcooled zone is uniform

- the exchange coefficient steam/pipes wall is constant

- the exchange coefficient liquid/pipes wall is constant

- absence of incondensable gases.

The model concerning the wall of tube bundle pipes is based on the following hypotheses:

- the thermal capacity of each cell is concentrated corresponding to its medium radius

- there is not heat conduction along the longitudinal direction

In the model of fluid inside tube bundle, it is assumed that all pipes in parallel configuration can be represented like only one equivalent pipe axially subdivided in two cells respectively faced with the cavity subcooled and condensing zones.

Inside the pipes, fluid is assumed with constant density and its heat conduction along the longitudinal direction negligible.

Inside the pipes, the fluid inertia is negligible; the fluid back-flow can not occur.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of mass in subcooled zone

2) the conservation equation of energy in subcooled zone

3) the conservation equation of mass in condensing zone

4) the conservation equation of energy in condensing zone

5) the energy conservation equation concerning the wall of tube bundle first cell

6) the energy conservation equation concerning the wall of tube bundle second cell

7) the fluid energy conservation equation inside the first cell of tube bundle

8) the fluid energy conservation equation inside the tube bundle second cell

9) the conservation equation of fluid momentum inside the tube bundle

10) the calculation equation of thermal power exchanged between the liquid inside cavity and the wall of tube bundle pipes

11) the calculation equation of thermal power exchanged between the steam inside cavity and the wall of the tube bundle pipes

12) the calculation equation of thermal power exchanged between the fluid inside the first cell of tube bundles and pipes wall

13) the calculation equation of thermal power exchanged between the fluid inside the second cell of tube bundle and pipes wall.

Among the above-mentioned equations, the first three are differential whereas the fourth one is algebraical since the accumulation term is not considered.

The equations 5), 6), 7), and 8) are differential but their related integration algorithm is directly implemented in the module so that they do not give rise to module residuals.

The variables they calculate are called internal states. To have the enthalpy of fluid flowing out from the bundle among the module output variables, such value is taken from the corresponding internal state by means of an algebraical equation.

The equations from 9) to 13) are algebraical. Since the thermal powers exchanged between the fluids and the pipes wall of tube bundle do not appear among the module output variables, the equations from 10) to 13) do not give rise to residuals.

Note 1

GAMC is a corrective factor which under stationary condition multiplies the thermal exchange total coefficient between steam inside the cavity and fluid flowing through the tube bundle.

Since under stationary condition both thermal exchange between steam and tube bundle metal and between tube bundle metal and fluid flowing through it are separately dealt with, such corrective factor both multiplies:

- the total coefficient of thermal exchange between the steam and the central zone of tube bundle metal

- the total coefficient of thermal exchange between the central zone of tube bundle metal and the fluid flowing through it.

If no initial value is assigned to GAMC, this is calculated in the module so that the thermal balance of condensing zone, deriving from the tentative values assigned to the other variables, is satisfied. Nevertheless if during calculation of a stationary condition, user has to determine the exchange coefficient related to a thermal balance or to a measured experimental situation, then GAMC must appear among the unknown of the system. In this case a module output variable must be declared NOTO (e.g. flow rate of steam flowing into the heater).

Note 2

GAMF is a corrective factor which, under stationary condition, multiplies the thermal exchange total coefficient between the liquid inside cavity and the fluid flowing through the tube bundle.

Since, under dynamic condition, both thermal exchange between the liquid inside the cavity and the tube bundle metal, and between the tube bundle metal and the fluid flowing through it are separately dealt with, such corrective factor both multiplies:

- the total coefficient of thermal exchange between the liquid inside the cavity and the central zone of tube bundle metal

- the total coefficient of thermal exchange between the central zone of tube bundle metal and the fluid flowing through it.

If no initial value is assigned to GAMF, this is calculated in the module so that it is satisfied the thermal balance of condensing zone, deriving from the tentative values assigned to the other variables. Nevertheless if during calculation of a stationary condition, user has to determine the exchange coefficient related to a thermal balance or to a measured experimental situation, then GAMF must appear among the unknown of the system. In this case a module output variable must be declared NOTO (e.g. enthalpy of liquid flowing out from the heater).

MODULE RSOR

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

To initialize the RSOR module variables no particular prescription is required.

It is useful to remember that (almost during the first phase of model set-up) it is suitable not to assign the values of the GAMC and GAMF corrective coefficients, because they are calculated by module itself also in case they were imposed NOTO.

CARD F 

Residuals

The module mathematical model consists of the equations mentioned in first paragraph, which give rise to the following residuals:

If WTUR = 0.

RNI(1) = QCON - WDRV*(HDRV - HLS)

(normalization factor = H0)

otherwise

RNI(1) = HTUR - HLS - [QCON - WDRV*(HDRV - HLS)]/WTUR

(normalization factor = H0)

RNI(2) = WTUR + WDIR - WCUR

(normalization factor = W0)

If WTUR = 0. and WDIR = 0.

RNI(3) = HLS - HLUR

(normalization factor = H0)

otherwise

RNI(3) = HLS - HLUR - [QSOT - WDRL*(HDRL - HLS)]/WCUR

(normalization factor = H0)

RNI(4) = HALI + (QCON + QSOT)/WALR - HALU

(normalization factor = H0)

RNI(5) = VSOT/SEZO - LIVR

(normalization factor = V0)

RNI(6) = PALI-PALU-(2.*ATTR*G²*(LCON+LSOT)/(DI*RF1)+9.90665*RF1*DELZ

(normalization factor = P0)

where:

HLS enthalpy of saturated liquid at cavity pressure

HVS enthalpy of saturated steam at cavity pressure

WDRL flow rate of drainages liquid component

HDRL enthalpy of drainages liquid component

WDRV flow rate of drainages steam component

HDRV enthalpy of drainages steam component

QCON power transferred to the tube bundle in condensing zone

QSOT power transferred to the tube bundle in subcooled zone

SEZO area of free mean horizontal section of subcooled zone

G specific mass flow rate of fluid inside tube bundle

DI hydraulic diameter of tube bundle pipes

RF1 density of fluid inside tube bundle

The remaining variables have the physical and geometrical meanings already defined.

Further explanations about quantities of the above-mentioned meanings are available in the section concerning the RSOR module of the manual "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".
