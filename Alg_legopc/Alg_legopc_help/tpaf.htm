MODULE TPAF

CARD A

Model Generalities

GAS TURBINE

 

The TPAF module simulates a gas turbine with the following features:

- the flow is evaluated by Stodola's law;

- the isentropic efficiency is depending on the expansion ratio;

- the thermal exchange between flue gas and metal of blades, rotor, stator and casing is supposed to be concentrated at the turbine inlet.

By the way, the thermal energy accumulation inside the metallic mass is not represented in TPAF module, but, if necessary, it must be taken into account by using another specific module.

The flue gas thermodynamic characteristics are obtained by calling the air and flue gas tables (TAVARFUM.FOR), based on the statistical theory of perfect gases, and considering the specific heat of each gas constituent the mixture as dependent on the temperature.

In the module the flue gas is treated as a real gas; the calculated results, therefore, depend on the relations utilized in the called tables (that means that real gases could be actually used by means of a simple substitution of the TAVARFUM tables with other ones at user's choice).

MODULE TPAF

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of TPAF module input and output variables, user must assign the following data set to complete the module input on the file F14:

[Pa] Pcam.nom Turbine inlet pressure in nominal conditions
[rad/s] Omeg nom Turbine rotation velocity in nominal conditions
[ ] nH/nC Hydrogen / Carbon molar ratio in the fuel; it must have the same value assigned in the CPAF combustion chamber
[ ] EtaS nom Turbine isentropic efficiency in nominal conditions
[ ] Coeff.K1 First coefficient of the turbine efficiency variation curve as a function of load (see note 1)
[ ] Coeff.K2

Second coefficient of the turbine efficiency variation curve as a function of load (see note 1)

Note 1

The turbine efficiency varies with the law:

Eta = EtaS Nom * (AK1 + (1. - AK1) / (1. + AK2 * (1. - PING/Pcam.nom)²))

User's Subprograms

No user subprograms are present in the TPAF module.

The TPAF module makes use of the procedures contained in the air and flue gas thermodynamic tables (TAVARFUM) which must be present in the user library.

MODULE TPAF

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the TPAF module mathematical model give rise to a set of residuals. Here only their correspondences with the output variables equations have been indicated, also mentioning the respective normalization factors. Further explanations are given in the manual section: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory - Modules Description".

1) RNI(1) - normalization factor = P0

related to the turbine suction flue gas pressure;

2) RNI(2) - normalization factor = T0

related to the turbine discharge flue gas temperature;

3) RNI(3) - normalization factor = W0 * H0

related to the mechanical power produced by the turbine;

4) RNI(4) - normalization factor = 1000.

related to the flue gas - metal exchange coefficient;
