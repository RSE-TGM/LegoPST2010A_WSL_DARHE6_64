MODULE CNDN

CARD A

Model Generalities

STEAM CONDENSER

 

The CNDN module simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena which the fluid present in the condenser cavity is subjected to. The simulation includes the thermal exchanges between the fluid in the cavity and the cooling fluid flowing through a tube bundle subdivided in NCEL cells having the same surface.

To define the cavity mathematical model the following simplifying hypotheses are considered:

- the thermal exchange with outside is negligible

- complete separation between steam and liquid phases

- thermodynamic equilibrium between the phases

- the pressure in the cavity is uniform

- the distribution of enthalpy in steam phase is uniform

- the distribution of enthalpy in liquid phase is uniform

- the exchange coefficient between the fluid and the tube bundle walls is constant

- absence of incondensable gas.

The used equations are:

1) the energy conservation equation of fluid contained in the cavity

2) the mass conservation equation of fluid contained in the cavity

3) the equation for liquid level calculation

4) the equation for pressure calculation on the condenser bottom

5) the equation for liquid level calculation as percentage of the measuring instruments working range.

 

To define the tube bundle mathematical model the following hypotheses are considered:

- the monodimensional approximation with concentrated parameters for energy conservation equation in the fluid

- the tube bundle discretized in NCEL axial cells having the same surface

- the liquid flowing through the subcooled tube bundle

- the pressure of fluid inside the tube bundle is constant

- the physical properties of tube bundle metal are constant

- the tube bundle pipes having thin thickness, so a discretization with only one radial cell is enough

- both in the metal and fluid the longitudinal conductivity of heat is negligible.

The used equations are:

6) the energy conservation equation in the fluid

7) the energy conservation equation in the metal.

The equation 5) can be defined both trough the data required by module and through the functions which user has to assign by an appropriate subprogram (see below).

The equations 6) and 7) are separately integrated and do not participate to the iterative process of general algorithm for system equation solution.

These equations contain inner module variables. Only two of them (respectively the fluid enthalpy and temperature at tube bundle outlet) are module output variables.

The CNDN module itself forms a complete model of steam condenser, so it can be used in place of the interconnected COND and TH2O modules.

Use of only one module in place of two, besides simplifying the achievement of a plant model, allows a saving in calculation time.

Nevertheless, acquisition of similar advantages is cause of precision drop; in fact, the CNDN module in spite of the COND module, can not suitably simulate flashing events.

Further informations about the two different models and their comparison are available in the section concerning this module of the manual "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".

MODULE CNDN

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables (meanings defined above), user must assign the following data:

[ ] NTUB number of pipes in the cooling tube bundle
[m] DIAE external diameter of cooling tube bundle pipes
[m] DIAI internal diameter of cooling tube bundle pipes
[m] LUNG length of cooling bundle pipes
[ ] TYPE_CAV selection index of cavity type to deal with (its value is assigned to the TYP variable, input parameters of the user program CNDLIV)
[m³] VOL.CAV. cavity internal volume (steam volume + liquid volume)
[m] ZERO_Y elevation gap between the condensate level, corresponding to the zero of measuring instruments, and the condensate outlet pipe
[m³] VOL_Y cavity volume between the zero of liquid level measuring instrument and the condensate outlet pipe
[m] GAMMA_Y working range of level measuring instruments (max. level over ZERO_Y)
[m²] SEZ_Y cavity horizontal cross section area
[kg/m³] ROM density of tube bundle metal
[J/kg/K] CP MET. specific heat of tube bundle metal
[W/m/K] COND.MET thermal conductivity of tube bundle metal
[ ] NCEL number of cells in which is discretized the tube bundle (max. 5).

To determine the condensate level, the CNDN module refers to a cavity where the liquid volume below the measuring instruments is known and level variation is proportional to liquid volume variation. So, it is a cavity where, within the working level range, the horizontal cross section area is constant. If user needs to refer to a more complex geometry of collection basin, data related to the new geometry can be supplied through the user subprogram SUBROUTINE CNDLIV(TYP, Y, VL, DVLDY, YPER) (see below). With an assigned liquid level, the subprogram calculates:

- the volume engaged by liquid as a function of the cavity geometrical characteristics

- the partial derivative of volume engaged by liquid evaluated as regards to the level

- the liquid proportional level as a function of the measuring instruments characteristic.

In this case, user has to assign to the SEZ_Y a negative or zero value. If this signal is found, the user subprogram is called and values of ZERO_Y, VOL_Y, GAMMA_Y, and SEZ_Y are not considered.

MODULE CNDN

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

To initialize the input and output variables of CNDN module, it needs to consider that in thermodynamic equilibrium hypotheses, once fixed the condensation pressure, the enthalpies of two phases are fixed too and, consequently the condensate enthalpy. Besides, if enthalpies and flow rates input are all known, power to give up to the cooler is fixed too.

On the basis of such considerations (beginning from the condensation pressure and presuming all enthalpies and flow rates at the cavity and tube bundle inlet are known) in the CNDNI2 subprogram are evaluated: -the two phase enthalpies -the pressure on the cavity bottom -the cooler enthalpy -the metal temperature at the tube bundle outlet.

So, to avoid inconsistencies with the above-mentioned calculations, user must assign and impose NOTO the values of condensation pressure and liquid level.

The remaining system values must not be imposed. To avoid the system to be over-constrained, user has to keep free two of the input variables and to impose NOTO to the remaining ones.

It is suggested to keep free the enthalpy of main turbine blow-off steam and condensate extraction flow rate.

CARD E 

User's Subprograms

If the collection basin has a more complex geometry than the reference one, in providing the related data to the module, user has to assign a negative or zero value to the SEZ_Y and write a user subprogram whose declarative statement is:

SUBROUTINE CNDLIV(TYP, Y, VL, DVLDY, YPER)

As a function of the liquid level, such program calculates:

- the engaged volume of liquid as a function of the cavity geometrical characteristics

- the partial derivative of volume engaged by the liquid, calculated as regards to the level

- the liquid proportional level as a function of the measuring instruments characteristics.

Input parameters: TYP, Y

Output parameters: VL, DVLDY, YPER

[ ] TYP  corresponds to the TYPE_CAV datum required in input (see above). It can be used as pointer to select the required cavity geometry in case user has to introduce different models of cavities into only one user subprogram
[m] Y liquid level inside the cavity
[m³] VL volume engaged by liquid
[m²] DVLDY derivative of volume engaged by liquid as regards to the level
[%] YPER liquid level in percentage of the measuring instruments working range.

 

Since cylindrical geometry having horizontal axis is one of the most usual geometry for drums, in the module the following user subprogram is available:

SUBROUTINE CNDLIO(Y, RCAV, HLCAV, VL, DVLDY)

which calculates (once assigned the level) the volume engaged by the liquid contained inside a cylinder having horizontal axis and the derivative of this volume as regards to the level.

The meanings of calling parameters are:

[m] Y level of liquid inside the cylinder
[m] RCAV cylinder internal radius
[m] HLCAV cylinder internal length
[m³] VL volume engaged by the liquid present inside the cylinder
[m²] DVLDY derivative of volume engaged by liquid as regards to the level

Thereinafter a CNDLIV subroutine example is indicated, using the CNDLIO subroutine, related to a cylindrical drum having radius RCAV = 1 m and length HLCAV = 10 m, whose level YPER is given as percentage of a range GAM = 2m.

SUBROUTINE CNDLIV(TYP, Y, VL, DVLDY, YPER)

IF(TYP.NE.1.) RETURN

RCAV=1.

HLCAV=10.

CALL CNDLIO(Y,RCAV,HLCAV,VL,DVLDY)

GAM=RCAV*2.

YPER=Y/GAM/100.

RETURN

END

SUBROUTINE CNDLIO(Y, RCAV, HLCAV, VL, DVLDY)

ALFA = ACOS((RCAV-Y)/RCAV)

VL = RCAV².*(ALFA-COS(ALFA)*SIN(ALFA))*HLCAV

DVLDY = 2.*RCAV*SIN(ALFA)*HLCAV

IF(DVLDY.LT..1E-5)DVLDY = 0.

RETURN

END

The residuals present in the module do not directly correspond to the equations mentioned above. Here only their respective normalization factors are indicated. Further explanations are given in the manual section "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory"

RNI(2) - normalization factor = 1.

RNI(3) - normalization factor = T0

RNI(4) - normalization factor = H0

RNI(5) - normalization factor = H0

RNI(6) - normalization factor = P0

RNI(7) - normalization factor = 1.
