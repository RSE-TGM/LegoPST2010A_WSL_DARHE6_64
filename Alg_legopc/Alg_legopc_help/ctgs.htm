MODULE CTGS

CARD A

Model Generalities

AXIAL COMPRESSOR FOR SYNGAS-FED TURBOGAS GROUP

 

The CTGS module simulates an axial compressor for turbogas; the physical-mathematical model used is based on the global characteristic curves of the machine. The module simulates the behaviour of the compressor also stage by stage or by groups of stages (delimited by the bleedings present in the machine) and not only its global behaviour.

The CTGS module allows the correct simulation of the bleedings present in the real compressors, of the channels of cooling of the turbine stages using air bleeded from different stages of the compressor, and of the blow-off valves.

The parameters using to define the global behaviour of the machine (only globally, and not stage by stage) have to be imposed by the user, assigning (using the related activity) data like the nominal inlet and outlet pressures and the nominal inlet air flow rate and temperature.

The coefficients which define the form of the characteristics curves of the machine, like the parameters of the interpolation curves of the pressure and the temperature for the single stages, are on the contrary directly defined inside the Fortran source of the module for two standard types of turbogas groups (the Fiat TG50D and the Siemens V94.3). For all the other types of group is possible to define such data using the related routine of the "auxiliary Fortran" (Foraus).

Note that for the Siemens V94.3 group (type 2) the module has been fully tested and validated as regards to the characteristics of the machine supplied by the manufacturer, while for the Fiat TG50D group (type 1) could be required a futher refining of the parameters.

For the calculation and the conversion of the thermodynamical properties of all the air flowing through the compressor is used the functions library devoted to the evaluation of the properties of gas mixtures (TAVSYNGAS library).

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

NSTADI = number of compressor stages

NSPILL = number of air bleedings

 

i = 1, NSTADI - 1

j = 1, NSPILL

 

Note: the flow rate in the first stage is called WASP instead of WST1 (the variable is present only for multi-stage compressors)

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[Pa] PASP Air pressure at the compressor inlet
[K] TASP Air temperature at the compressor inlet
[Pa] PMAN Air pressure at the compressor outlet
[RPM] RPMC Rotational speed of the compressor
[deg] AIGV Rotation angle of the compressor IGV
[%] ZSPO Gritness percentage of the compressor blades
[kg/s] WSPj Air flow rate in the j-th bleeding of the compressor
[kg/s] WMAN Air flow rate at the compressor outlet
[K] TMAN Air temperature at the compressor outlet
[W] QCOM Mechanical power absorbed by the compressor
[kg/s] WSTi Air flow rate in the i-th stage of the compressor
[Pa] PCAi Air pressure in the i-th cavity of the compressor
[K] TCAi Air temperature in the i-th cavity of the compressor

Note: the flow rate in the first stage is called WASP instead of WST1 (the variable is present only for multi-stage compressors)

 

INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES NORMALIZATION FACTORS

Variables Normalization
PASP, PMAN, PCAi P0
TASP, TMAN, TCAi T0
WSPj, WMAN, WASP, WSTi W0
QCOM  W0 * H0
RPMC 300.
AIGV, ZSPO 100.

MODULE CTGS

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of CTGS module input and output variables, user must assign the following data set to complete the module input on the file F14:
[kg/s] W nom. Inlet air flow rate in nominal conditions
[Pa] Pi nom. Inlet air pressure in nominal conditions
[Pa] Pu nom. Outlet air pressure in nominal conditions
[K] Ti nom. Inlet air temperature in nominal conditions
[RPM] RPM nom. Nominal rotational speed of the compressor
[ ] Eta nom. Isoentropic efficiency of the compressor in nominal conditions
[ ] TG type

Type of Turbogas group:

1 = Fiat TG50D

2 = Siemens V94.3
[%] Rid.Eta% Maximum reduction in percentage of the efficiency due to the compressor blades gritness
[ ] Bleedn.i Number of the cavity of the j-th bleeding (j = 1, NSPILL)

Note: no prescription is given about the number of bleeding in each cavity or the order of the bleedings (they may be or don’t be sorted).

MODULE CTGS

CARD E 

User's Subprograms

In the auxiliary Fortran (Foraus) of the CTGS module there is only one routine, called CTGSDU, whose declaratory instructions are the followings:

SUBROUTINE CTGSDU (ITIPO, IER, NSTADI, CW, CE, CI, CS, CB)

DIMENSION CW(5), CE(4), CI(3), CS(4,5), CB(3,9)

The parameters have the following meanings:

* ITIPO [read-only] type of compressor (turbogas group) assigned by the user
* IER [write-only] IER parameter of the CTGSI2 subroutine - to be set to 1 in case of errors, in order to abort the data acquisition activity
* NSTADI [read-only] number of stages of the compressor (to be used for the check of the coherence between the value assigned in the present routine for the selected type of compressor and the value assigned by the user during topology building phase)
* CW [write-only] coefficients a0, a2, a3, b1 and b2 for the definition of the characteristics curves of the compressor (see theoretical related description)
* CE [write-only] coefficients k1, k2, keta and kpot for the definition of the efficiency variation curves and corrective correlated factors (see theoretical related description)
* CI [write-only] coefficients ci0, ci1 and ci2 for the definition of the flow rate variation curves of the ground of the IGV position (see theoretical related description)
* CS [write-only]

matrix of the parameters c0, c1, c2 and xsup for the definition of the surge curve (up to a maximum of 5 parable branches; see theoretical related description)
* CB [write-only] matrix of the coefficients cb1, cb2 and cb3 for the definition of the variation curves of the pressure in the single cavities of the compressor (the number of rows to fill is equal to the number of stages decreased of one; see theoretical related description)

Also the CTGS module makes use of the procedures contained in the syngas thermodynamic tables (TAVSYNGAS) which must be present in the user library.

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the CTGS module mathematical model (mentioned above) give rise to a set of residuals. Here only their correspondences with the output variables equations have been indicated, also mentioning the respective normalization factors. Further explanations are given in the related theoretical description.

1) RNI(1) - normalization factor = W0

related to the air flow rate at the compressor outlet

2) RNI(2) - normalization factor = T0

related to the air temperature at the compressor outlet

3) RNI(3) - normalization factor = W0 * H0

related to the mechanical power absorbed by the compressor

do i = 1, NSTADI - 1

4) RNI(3*i+1) - normalization factor = W0

related to the air flow rate in the i-th stage

5) RNI(3*i+2) - normalization factor = P0

related to the air pressure inside the i-th cavity

6) RNI(3*i+3) - normalization factor = T0

related to the air temperature inside the i-th cavity

enddo
