MODULE EVAP

CARD A

Model Generalities

TWO PHASES (steam_water) EXCHANGER - HOMOGENEOUS MODEL

 

The EVAP module simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena which water is subjected to, when it flows in a channel with metallic walls able to exchange heat with outside.

The module allows the simulation of one or more piping (in series or parallel configuration), or plant components whose geometry can be assimilated to an equivalent number of channels in parallel configuration.

It particularly meets the simulation of connection piping, heat exchanger tube bundles, or other components like steam generators in which compressible fluids flow through. In the latter case, besides simulating appropriately physical phenomena like the natural circulation, the module input-output scheme is compatible with those of the CCMP (combustion furnace) and FUMN (flue gas duct).

The module mathematical model is developed under the following hypotheses:

- for the mass, energy and momentum conservation equations, the monodimensional approximation with concentrated parameters is considered valid (the energy, mass and momentum balances are carried out for each single cell)

- the channel is subdivided, (along its axial direction), in an arbitrary number of cells (from 2 to 9) which can (or not) exchange heat with outside - the water flowing through the channel is considered as compressible fluid and can undergo some phase transitions. So, it can be in liquid state, or as steam as well as a liquid-steam two phase mixture

- in case of two phase mixture, as physic-mathematical model is adopted the homogeneous model without slip between the two phases - in the momentum conservation equation, the water inertia is considered as negligible

- it is assumed that the flow rate of water inside the channel can be inverted both on inlet and outlet sides - dealing with the thermal exchanges between the water flowing in the channel and the flue gas licking its external wall, the wall thickness is considered thin as regards to the diameter

- the temperature distribution on the external surface of each cell is assumed as uniform

- each cell is subdivided (along axial direction) in a certain number of thermal exchange zones (for each cell a maximum number of 6 zones is allowed with a further restriction concerning the total number of zones which must not exceed 32). In each zone the fluid licking the cell external surface is characterized by its own mean temperature

- the heat longitudinal conduction is considered as negligible.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of water mass

2) the conservation equation of water momentum

3) the conservation equation of energy in the water

4) the conservation equation of energy in metallic walls

5) the thermal exchange equation between the channel external surface and the fluid licking it

6) the equation for enthalpy calculation of water at the channel outlet

7) the equation for enthalpy calculation of water at the channel inlet when back-flow occurs.

The conservation equation of energy in the channel metallic walls and the conservation equation of energy in the water flowing in the channel are integrated directly inside the module and formally presented as algebraical equations.

The external superficial thermal exchange coefficient between the metallic walls and the licking fluid is a module input variable. On the contrary, the internal exchange coefficient between the metallic walls and the water flowing through the channel is calculated by means of a correlation selected by the user in a suitable set (see below).

If the piping (or tube bundle) to simulate has concentrated head losses (as, for example, elbows or reducing fittings), they have to be distributed on the whole piping incorporating them in an equivalent friction coefficient. The value of such coefficient can be calculated, during stationary evaluation, assigning suitable boundary conditions.

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

NCEL = number of cells (NCEL = 2, ..., 9)

NZ(i) = number of thermal exchange zones in which the i-th cell is subdivided (i = 1, 2, ..., NCEL)

The module can be interconnected with other modules according to three methods:

 

1) output in inlet and outlet pressure

prescription about block name: none

 

 

2) output in inlet flow rate and outlet pressure

prescription about block name: preceded by '+' sign

 

 

3) output in inlet and outlet flow rate

prescription about block name: preceded by '-' sign

[Pa] PCi (i = 1, 2, ..., NCEL) mean pressure of the fluid in the i-th channel cell
[kg/s] WGi (i = 1, 2, ..., NCEL-1) flow rate of the fluid flowing out from the i-th channel cell
[Pa] PUTU channel outlet pressure
[Pa] PITU channel inlet pressure

[J/kg] HUTU fluid enthalpy at the channel outlet
[J/kg] HIIT fluid enthalpy at the channel inlet when back-flow occurs
[J/kg] HITU fluid enthalpy at the channel inlet
[J/kg] HUIT fluid enthalpy at the channel outlet when back-flow occurs
[kg/s] WUTU fluid flow rate at the channel outlet
[kg/s] WITU fluid flow rate at the channel inlet
[K] TFij (i = 1, 2, ..., NCEL) (j = 1, 2, ...NZ(i)) temperature of the fluid licking the external surface of the i-th cell in the j-th zone
[K] GFij (i = 1, 2, ..., NCEL) (j = 1, 2, ...NZ(i)) superficial thermal exchange coefficient between the fluid licking the external surface of the i-th cell in the j-th zone and cell wall metal
[ ] ATTR channel friction coefficient (see note 2)

Note 1

The thermal power exchanged between the external surface of the i-th channel cell and the fluid licking it is determined by means of the following relation:

QTi = <GFi>*Si*[<TFi> - TMi] [i = 1,..., NCEL]

where:

Si is the exchange total surface
<GFi> is the mean value of the superficial thermal exchange coefficient between the fluid licking the cell external surface and the surface itself
<TFi> is the temperature mean value of the fluid in the cell.

So the power QTi is positive when fluid gives up heat to the surface.

MODULE EVAP

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

 

In addition to the initial value of input and output variables (meanings defined above), user must assign the following data:

Data concerning the whole channel:

[kg/m³] ROM density of channel metallic wall
[J/kg/K] CM specific heat of channel metallic wall
[W/m/K] COND 

 thermal conductivity of channel metallic wall.

Data concerning the single cells:

[ ] NTUB number of channels in parallel configuration
[m] DIAI internal diameter of channels in parallel configuration
[m] DIAE external diameter of channels in parallel configuration
[m] LUNG length of channels in parallel configuration
[m] DELZ geodetic gap between the channel inlet and outlet (positive when the channel outlet is at higher level than inlet)
[ ] CRIG selection parameter of mathematical model to use in determining the convective thermal exchange coefficient between the channel wall metal and the fluid flowing through it. Depending from the assigned value to this parameter, several operative options are carried out according to the following scheme:
CRIG < 0. correlation for the exchange coefficient calculation. It must be provided by user through the subprogram SUBROUTINE GAMUTXE(PC, H, S, T, RO, TIT, TMC, DIAI, CRIG, G, QT, SUP, GAMIN) (see below)
CRIG = 0. correlations used for the exchange coefficient calculation. It only takes into account the thermo-dynamic state of fluid flowing through the channel. Such correlations are directly encoded inside the module in the subroutine GAMTERT
CRIG > 0. correlations used for the thermal coefficient calculation. They are encoded in the subprogram of library SUBROUTINE GAM01. They take into account both thermo-dynamic state of fluid flowing through the channel and channel wall temperature. Besides, a distinction is made between channels having smooth or rough surfaces.

The selection between the two different channels is carried out as a function of the value assigned to the CRIG parameter which, in this case, also identifies a multiplying coefficient "MOLT" which changes the value of exchange coefficient calculated by correlations:

0.> CRIG > 10. - smooth channels

MOLT = CRIG

10.> CRIG > 20. - rough channels

if CRIG = 10, 11 ==> MOLT = 1.

otherwise MOLT = CRIG - 10.

User has to take into account that most accuracy as regards to the use of sub-routine GAMTERT (acquired in the exchange coefficient calculation using sub-routine GAM01) is to calculation time detriment. In fact, the calculation times for the GAM01 are more or less the double of those needed for GAMERT. Further informations about contents and use methods of the subroutine GAM01 are in the related section of the manual "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Module Description".

[m³]  VOL.M.Ci volume of metal forming the i-th cell walls
[m²] SUP.j Ci exchange surface area between external fluid and channel wall in the j-th zone of the i-th cell.

When several consecutive cells have the same geometrical data, it is enough to assign those related to the first cell. The same data are automatically assigned to the following cells.

User's Subprograms

 

To introduce in the module a correlation giving the convective thermal exchange coefficient between the channel internal wall and the fluid flowing through the channel, user must assign a negative value to CGIG (see above) and write a user subprogram whose declarative statement is:

    SUBROUTINE GAMUTXE(PC, H, S, T, RO, TIT, TMC, DIAI, CRIG, G, QT,

    $ SUP, GAMIN)

 

Input parameters: PC, H, S, T, RO, TIT, TMC, DIAI, CRIG, G, QT, SUP

Output parameters: GAMIN

[Pa] PC mean pressure of fluid flowing through the channel
[J/kg] H specific enthalpy of fluid flowing through the channel
[J/kg/K] S specific entropy of fluid flowing through the channel
[K] T mean temperature of fluid flowing through the channel
[kg/m³] RO mean density of fluid flowing through the channel
[ ] TIT steam ratio of fluid flowing through the channel
[K] TMC mean temperature of channel wall metal
[m] DIAI channel internal diameter
[ ] CRIG parameter corresponding to the CRIG data described above. It can be used as selection index of the correlation to use in the convective exchange coefficient calculation between fluid and metal, when user has to include several correlations in only one subroutine GAMUTXE
[kg/m²/s] G specific flow rate of the fluid flowing through the channel
[W] QT thermal power exchanged between the wall metal and the fluid flowing through the channel
[m²] SUP exchange surface area between the wall metal and the fluid flowing through the channel
[W/m²/K] GAMIN convective thermal exchange coefficient between the wall metal and the fluid flowing through the channel.

Hereinafter it is indicated an example of the subroutine GAMUTXE related to an assignment of a constant value to the GAMIN exchange coefficient and to its calculation by means of Dittus-Bölter's correlation as dependence of the value assigned to CRIG:

    SUBROUTINE GAMUTXE(PC,H,S,T,RO,TIT,TMC,DIAI,CRIG,G,QT,SUP,GAMIN)

C

C

    REAL PC,H,S,T,RO,TIT,TMC,DIAI,CRIG,G,QT,SUP,GAMIN

C

    INTEGER ICO

C

    ICO=-CRIG

    GO TO (1,2) ICO

C

C*************************** CONSTANT **********************************

C

    1 GAMIN=65000.

    RETURN

C

    2 CONTINUE

C

C*************************** DITTUS ************************************

C

    GAMIN=DITTUS (G,DIAI,PC,T,S,RO,TIT)

C

    RETURN

    END

 

    FUNCTION DITTUS (G,D,P,T,S,RO,TIT)

    REAL MU,K

C

C   READING FROM THERMODYNAMIC PROPERTIES TABLES

C

    CALL SATUR(P,3,ROLS,ROVS,1)

C

    ROLG=ROLS/ROVS

C

    CP=CPEV(P,S,TIT,1.,1.)

    MU=ETEV(P,T,RO,ROLG,TIT,1.,1.)

    K=ALEV(P,T,RO,ROLG,TIT,1.,1.)

C

C   REYNOLDS' NUMBER

C

    RE=G*D/MU

    IF(RE.LT.10000.) RE=10000.

C

C   PRANDTL'S NUMBER

C

    PR=CP*MU/K

C

C   EXCHANGE COEFFICIENT CALCULATION

C

    DITTUS=0.023*(K/D)*RE**.8*PR**.4

    RETURN

    END

Residuals

The residuals present in the module directly correspond to the equations mentioned in the first paragraph. Here only the correspondences between residuals and equations are indicated, also mentioning their respective normalization factors. Further explanations are given in the related section of the manual: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory - Modules Description"

I = 1, NCEL

1) RNI(I) - normalization factor = P0

are related, cell by cell, to the mass balance of the water flowing through the channel

J = 1, NCEL+1

2) RNI(NCEL+J) - normalization factor = DP0

are related, cell by cell, to the momentum balance of the water flowing through the

channel

K = 1, NCEL

3) RNI(2*NCEL+1+K) - normalization factor = T0

are related, cell by cell, to the energy balance in the channel metal wall

L = 1, NCEL

4) RNI(3*NCEL+1+L) - normalization factor = W0*H0

are related, cell by cell, to the thermal exchange equation between the external surface of channel wall and the fluid licking it

5) RNI(4*NCEL+2) - normalization factor = H0

is related to the enthalpy calculation equation of water at the channel outlet

6) RNI(4*NCEL+2) - normalization factor = W0*H0

is related to the enthalpy calculation equation of water at the channel inlet, when back-flow occurs
