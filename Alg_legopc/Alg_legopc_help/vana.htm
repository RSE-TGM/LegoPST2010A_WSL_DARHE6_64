MODULE VANA

CARD A

Model Generalities

OIL VALVE

 

The VANA module can simulate the operation of a piping with valve, for flowing oil or naphtha.

 

Main hypothesis at the module basis are:

- fluid flowing has the same density in whole piping: the relevant value depends on enthalpy value at the piping input.

- thermodynamic transformation undergone by the fluid inside is isoenthalpic;

- both thermal and mass accumulation inside valve and piping are neglected;

- piping has constant cross section;

- heat loss across the walls of the system is considered

 

The valve position inside the piping is neglectable since fluid properties inside the same are constant along the piping.

For the assumptions made, the flow rate in the piping is constant along the piping.

 

Equations in mathematical model are:

- the equation of momentum conservation in the whole piping, with calculation of load loss in the piping and in the valve. Also gravitational effect are taken into account.

- the conservation equation of the energy, taking into account the heat exchange with the external environment.

Topological data

INPUT - OUTPUT SCHEME

 

The module can be interconnected to other modules according to three patterns:

 

1) mass flow rate as output variable

prescription on block name: none

 

2) upstream pressure output

prescription on lock name: '+' put before

 

 

3) downstream pressure output

prescription on lock name: '-' put before

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[Pa] PIVN pressure upstream piping
[Pa] PUVN pressure downstream piping
[K] TVNA oil temperature upstream the piping
[K] TVIN oil temperature upstream the piping
(in case of flow rate inversion)
[K] TEXT temperature of external environment
[p.u.] ALZA valve lift
[ ] CORR correction for friction factor in the piping
[kg/s] WVNA fluid mass flow rate
[K] TNUV outlet oil temperature

Note : if one of two pressure output configurations is employed, the valve lift is no longer effective: the model put its value at one, whatever may be the value imposed as input. Furthermore, the option for check valve (IND.NRIT = 1) has no effect.

MODULE VANA

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

 

Meaning of data required at input by the VANA module:

Data relevant to the valve:

[ ] ALFA coefficient used to define the valve characteristic (section vs. lift)
[ ] CFA (currently not used)
[m²] AREA actual fluid flow area in the valve when it is fully open (if no valve is installed on the piping, no value shall be assigned)

Data relevant to piping:

[m] LUNGHEZZ piping length
[m] DIAMETRO piping diameter
[m] DISLIVEL difference in level between piping input and output
[W/K] GAM*SUP exchange coefficient times exchange surface

Data relevant to the type of oil and presence of a check valve:

[ ] TIPONAFT index identifying the type of oil present in the piping (for oil classification see documentation relevant to "naphtha properties")
[ ] IND.NRIT index relevant to the presence of the check valve:
0 = check valve not present
1 = check valve present
This index has effect only with mass flow rate as output.
[m³] VOLUME volume of the duct
If it is zero and LUNGHEZZ is not zero, the default value is the volume of cylindrical pipe, with specified diameter and lenght.

CARD F 

Residuals

Residuals in the module:

RNI(1)=

PIVN-PUVN+DPB-DPD-DPV

(norm. factor P0)

RNI(2)=

DT - TUSC - TPREC 

(norm.factor T0)

Variables appearing at the second member have the following meaning:

PIVN pressure at piping input
PUVN pressure at piping output
DPB pressure variation due to difference in level between input and output
DPD load loss along piping (except for valve)
DPV load loss due to the valve
TUSC temperature of the fluid at the system outlet
TPREC TUSC value at one time step before
DT temperature variation during the last time step, calculated by the conservation of energy

DT has the following expression:

DT = DTINT * (ABS(W)*(HMS-HNAF)+GS*(TEXT-TUSC))/(RO*VOL*CP)

where:

DTINT time step amplitude
W mass flow rate
HMS enthalpy of entering flow
HNAF mixing enthalpy
GS product between external exchange coefficient and surface
TEXT external temperature
TUSC outlet and mixing temperature
RO, CP fluid density and specific heat
VOL volume of valve and piping vanes

In steady state the second residual has a similar but simpler form:

RNI(2) = (TUSC-TEXT)/T0

IF (ABS(W).GT.W0*1.E-5)THEN

    RNI(2) = (W*(HMS-HNAF) + GS*(TEXT-TUSC)) / (W0*H0)

ENDIF
