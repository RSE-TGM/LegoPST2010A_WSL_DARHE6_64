MODULE MITN

CARD A

Model Generalities

N-FLUIDS THERMAL MIXER

 

The MITN module simulates the perfect mixing of "N" fluids flowing into a cavity.

Simplifying hypotheses adopted in the physical-mathematical model definition are the following:

- absence of thermal exchange of the "N" fluids with the surface of the cavity;

- absence of phenomena of mass and energy accumulation in the cavity;

The fluids incoming from the different pipes must be omogeneous (constituted by the same kind of fluid) and in the same phisical state, as the module doesn’t evaluate any phisical state transition.

The specific heat of the fluid is assumed as constant (independent from the temperature).

The difference between the MIXN and the MITN module is that the MIXN module is an enthalpic mixer (inputs and output in enthalpies) and it is devoted to the simulation of water and/or steam mixers, while the MITN is a thermal mixer (inputs and output in temperatures) and it is devoted to the simulation of gases, oils and any other fluids treated by LEGOCAD System by temperature.

The used equations, both algebraical are:

1) the energy conservation equation

2) the mass conservation equation

The physical-mathematical model inside the module is not able to simulate in a satisfying manner cases of back-flow. If during the simulation of any transient the back-flow of any flows should happen (although simulation is not interrupted), the obtained quantity trends may be little reliable. So it is necessary to check that during the transient evolution this situation does not take place.

MODULE MITN

CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

N = number of input branches in the cavity (max. 10)

i = 1, 2, ... N

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WMIX flow rate of fluid flowing out of the cavity
[K] TMIX temperature of fluid flowing out of the cavity
[kg/s] WE_i (i = 1, 2, ... N) flow rate of fluid flowing into the cavity from the i-th branch
[K] TE_i (i = 1, 2, ... N) temperature of fluid flowing into the cavity from the i-th branch
