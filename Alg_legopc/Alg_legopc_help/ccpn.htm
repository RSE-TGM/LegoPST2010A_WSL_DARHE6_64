MODULE CCPN

CARD A

Model Generalities

BOILER FURNACE 0-D MODEL FOR GAS CIRCUIT

 

The CCPN module simulates the gas pressure dynamics in a boiler which is schematized as only one cavity where a accumulation mass phenomenon can occur.

The model is based on the following main hypotheses:

- the distribution of pressure in the cavity is uniform

- the validation of perfect gas law for fluid in the cavity

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of fluid mass in the cavity

2) the state equation of perfect gas.

In order to take into account the actual gas temperature distribution from combustion furnace to second gas pass, the cavity total volume (Vtot) is subdivided in cells having volume Vi, where the gas has a mean cell temperature Ti.

The Ti temperatures distribution must be known, so it must be assigned or calculated by other modules simulating the thermal exchange in the boiler. For the temperatures distribution inside the combustion furnace and inside the second pass gas, the CCPN/CCMN and CCPN/FUMN modules have to be interconnected respectively. In both cases the boiler cells subdivision for the CCMP and FUMN modules must be the same used for the CCPN.

The mathematical models of CCMP and FUMN consist of only algebraical equations.

Under sudden transient conditions like "boiler shut down" where, because of simultaneous blowing-out of all burners a sudden temperature drop occurs, the CCMN and FUMN modules are not able to follow the dynamics of such temperature and instantaneously give their rated value. To obtain a correct simulation of pressure dynamics in similar transients, in the CCPN module the acquisition of temperature variations (given by the CCMP and FUMN modules) is made with a certain delay. The following relation gives, for each cell, the temperature T(n) actually used for pressure calculation at the t-n time interval:

T(n) = T(n-1)*exp(-Dt/TAU) + ^T(n)*[1.- exp(-Dt/TAU)]

where:

- T(n-1) is the temperature calculated in the preceding time interval

- Dt is the integration time interval

- TAU is the time constant

- ^T(n) is the temperature calculated by the CCMP or FUMN modules at the t-n interval. The above-mentioned relation is deduced by the discretization of the time constant.....equation TAU, according to the Z transform method.

Further information are available in the section concerning this module of the manual "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants-Theory".

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg] MFUM mass of flue gas inside the boiler
[Pa] PFUM pressure of flue gas inside the boiler
[kg/s] WC(I) I = 1, N_C_BRU flow rate of fuel inside the boiler i-th cell
[kg/s] WA(I) I = 1, N_C_BRU flow rate of comburent air inside the boiler i-th cell

[kg/s] WFUC flow rate of flue gas at the boiler exit
[K] TF(J) J = 1, NUM_CEL mean temperature of flue gas inside the boiler j-th cell

MODULE CCPN

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

[ ] NUM_CEL. (max. 30.) number of cells in which the boiler is subdivided
[ ] N_C_BRU. (max. 5.) number of cells equipped with burners
[m³] VOLCEL(J) volume of the boiler i-th cell (see note 1)
[ ] IBRUC_(I) corresponding index between cells and burners (see note 2)
[s] TAU_1 time constant used for the flue gas temperatures calculation inside the cells not equipped with burners (convective area). Usual value TAU_1 = 6.5 s
[s] TAU_2 time constant used for the flue gas temperatures calculation inside the cells equipped with burners (radiated area). Usual value TAU_2 = 0.5 s

Note 1

If the number of cells subdividing the boiler is lower than the maximum allowed (30.), the fields VOLCEL(J) related to not foreseen cells must be empty.

Note 2

The simulation foresees a maximum of 5 cells equipped with burners. In the IBRUC_(I) fields user specifies, in a progressive order, the number of cell equipped with burners. If the number of equipped cells is lower than the maximum allowed, the remaining fields must be empty. E.g. if the cells total number is 6 and the cells 2 and 3 are only equipped with burners, user must indicate:

IBRUC_1 = 2., IBRUC_2 = 3., IBRUC_(I = 3, 4, 5) = 0.

It is an error to indicate: IBRUC_1 = 3., IBRUC_2 = 2.

MODULE CCPN

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

To initialize the CCPN module variables no particular prescription is required. In the cells equipped with burners, the air total flow rate flowing into the cell and the fuel total flow rate related to the lighted burners are required.

Residuals

The equations forming the mathematical model give rise to the following residuals:

 

The residual is normalized as regards to the mass AM0 = 1.E+05 kg.

where RGAS is the constant used in the perfect gas law (see note).

The residual is normalized as regards to pressure PRIF = 1.E+05 Pa.

Note

The perfect gas law is expressed by the following state equations:

P*V = n*R*T

where:

- P is the gas pressure

- V is the gas volume

- T is the gas temperature

- R is the gas universal constant

- n is the mole number of considered gas

 

Dividing by gas mass M we get:

P/RO = (n*R/M)*T

where RO is the gas density.

Putting:

with M/n gas molecular mass we get:

P/RO = RGAS*T
