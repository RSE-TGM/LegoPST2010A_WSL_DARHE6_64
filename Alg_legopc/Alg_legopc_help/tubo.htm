CARD B

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

The module can be interconnected with other ones according to the following three methods:

1) output in flow rate

prescription about block name: none

2) outlet in inlet pressure

prescription about block name: preceded by a '+' sign

3) output in outlet pressure

prescription about block name: preceded by a '-' sign

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WTUB water flow rate inside the piping
[Pa] PITU pressure upstream the piping
[Pa] PUTU pressure downstream the piping
[J/kg] HTUB water enthalpy at the piping inlet
[ ] AKTB corrective factor of piping friction coefficient (see equation (1) above)

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

[kg/s] PORTATA water flow rate in the piping
[Pa] P1 MONTE pressure upstream the piping
[Pa] P2 VALLE pressure downstream piping
[J/kg] ENTALPIA water enthalpy at the piping inlet
[ ] COEFF head loss coefficient (see equation (1) above)
[m] DISLIV. geodetic gap between the piping inlet and outlet (positive if the piping outlet elevation is higher than the inlet one)

Note

The COEFF piping head loss coefficient must only be assigned when the data related to a known condition are not available (flow rate, upstream and downstream pressures, enthalpy).
