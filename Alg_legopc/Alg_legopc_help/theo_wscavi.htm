When condensation takes place the arrival rate exceeds the departure rate and during evaporation the reverse occurs. Non-equilibrium cavities with air (uncondensable gas)

Modules referred to: CLAV

The main hypotheses are the same as for the other non-equilibrium cavities, namely:

* the cavity is split in two zones: the lower zone, named liquid zone, contains liquid or low quality mixture, and the upper one, named gas zone, contains gas or high quality mixture

* the two zones can be in thermal non-equilibrium

* no heat transfer occurs between fluid and the cavity wall.

The additional hypotheses are:

* steam and air occupy the same volume

* steam and air have the same temperature

* no heat transfer between air and liquid zone fluid

* the total pressure is the sum of air and steam partial pressures

* air is a perfect gas

With regard to the interface heat transfer Q evaluation the same considerations hold as in the case with no presence of a non-condensable component. It must be noted that the presence of even a small quantity of non-condensable gas in the condensing vapour has a great influence on the resistance to heat transfer in the region of the liquid-vapour interface. The non-condensable gas is carried with the vapour towards the interface where it accumulates, and the partial pressure of the gas at the interface increases above that in the bulk of the mixture. Since the total pressure remains constant the partial pressure of vapour at the interface is lower that in the bulk mixture.
