MODULE ASCA

CARD A

Model Generalities

SIMPLIFIED EXCHANGER

The ASCA module represents the pattern of the operation of a water-water exchanger with some simplifications.

Let’s consider a plant component where water flows from both branches, for instance, process water from one branch and cooling water from the other one.

The model shall consider thermal capacities of the two fluid masses contained in the exchanger in addition to currents.

The possibility of a heat loss towards the outside is also considered on the cooler side.

The thermal exchange is treated according to the following hypothesis:

- Assuming as known the performance of the exchanger at its rated operation point, that is, given the two mass flow rates, obtain the product of global exchange coefficient by exchange surface between the two currents;

- The exchanger dimensioning in that point is the optimum one, that is the thermal resistance obtained from the reciprocal of the above mentioned product is shared in two perfectly identical parts by the two branches.

- The exchange coefficient in each branch depends only on the relevant mass flow rate, and more particularly the same is proportional to this one increased to 0.8.

Known the above mentioned conditions and formulated the hypothesis as above, it is possible to calculate, obtaining temperatures from enthalpies (it is known that the fluid is water), the power exchanged, knowing mass flow rates only.

Keeping into account power stored, the module can be used also in case one or both the mass flow rates are nullified.

The exchange with the outside of the cooler side, finaly allows to simulate the realistic cooling of the system during its non use.

Therefore, knowing mass flow rates and input conditions for both the branches, as well as surface, temperature and outside exchange coefficient, and masses contained in the two branches, we could describe the behaviour of the system with two energy conservation differential equations, supported by two calculations of lminal exchange coefficient, or thermal resistances to be summed up and of power exchanged with the outside.

The two equations are seen by the user as algebraic ones, but they are actually treated as differential inside the module. A final comment concerns pressures: on the cooler side it is assumed to be atmospheric, on the opposite side faculty is given to assign a value; in any case pressure is used only for the calculation of water properties and has neglectable influence.

MODULE ASCA

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables of the ASCA module, whose meaning has been defined in card B, the user must assign the following set of data to complete the input required by the module:

[kg/s] WNRE Rated capacity cooler side
[kg/s] WNSE Rated capacity cooled side

[W/K] GAMSUPNOM Surface rated product by global exchange coefficient
[kg] MASSLREF Fluid mass contained in cooler side

[kg] MASSLSER Fluid mass contained in cooled side
[m²] SUPEXT Exchange surface with the outside

C

RNI(1) = (WFSE * (HISE - HUSE) - Q)/(H0*W0)

C

C

RNI(2) = (WFRE * (HURE - HIRE) - Q + QEXT)/(H0*W0)

C

ELSE

C

RNI(1) = (HSPR - HUSE + DHSDT * DTINT)/H0

C

RNI(2) = (HRPR - HURE + DHRDT * DTINT)/H0

C

ENDIF

DHSDT and DHRDT are the derivatives of enthalpies with respect to time, calculated inside the module, while DTINT is the integration time step, while in steady state conditions, presence of Q and QEXT, powers exchanged between the two branches and the environment are noticed.
