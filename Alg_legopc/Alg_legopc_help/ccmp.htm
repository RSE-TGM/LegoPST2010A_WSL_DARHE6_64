MODULE CCMP

CARD A

Model Generalities

BOILER FURNACE 1-D MODEL

The CCMP module simulates the functioning of a boiler combustion furnace fed by fossil fuels (fuel oil, coal, natural gas).

It particularly simulates the thermodynamic behaviour of fluid present on the "air/flue gas side" of the combustion furnace.

In order to realize a complete combustion furnace model, user must interconnect the CCMP and EVAP modules. EVAP simulates the thermo-hydraulic behaviour of water flowing through the tube bundles forming the combustion furnace walls and the influence that pipes metal has in the thermal exchange between flue gas and water.

The physic-mathematical model, encoded in the CCMP, is referred to a radiance boiler equipped with or without "NOx-ports". The combustion furnace is subdivided in "NCEL" axial cells (3<NCEL<10) (see fig.1). In every cell the flue gas exchanges heat with "NPi" (max. 9) (see note 1) surfaces having different temperatures. The combustion stoichiometric balance in each cell is based on the fuel and air quantities actually present inside the cell taking into account the flow rates introduced from outside as well as coming from the preceding cell (if stoichiometrically exceeding).

For the NBRUC cells (1<NBRUC<6) (see note 2) the presence of several burners is foreseen. They are represented in each NBRUC cell as only one equivalent burner. To each equivalent burner user must assign a training arc (only for the boiler equipped with tangential burner it may be different than zero). The used fuel can be gas, fuel oil or coal.

Further hypotheses introduced in developing the physic-mathematical model, encoded in this module, are the following:

- the monodimensional approximation with concentrated parameters is valid for the conservation equations of fluid mass and energy inside the combustion furnace

- links between the input and output quantities from control volumes (coinciding with the cell volumes), for which the conservation equations are written, are instantaneous (static model). In other words in the conservation equations the accumulation terms are not considered, so equations themselves are algebraical and not differential.

- To deal with the energetic exchanges the following thermal exchange mechanisms are considered:

a) the convective thermal exchange between the fluid flowing through the cell and the NPi cell exchange surfaces.

b) The radiative thermal exchange between the fluid flowing through the cell and the NPi cell exchange surfaces.

c) The radiative thermal exchange between the fluid flowing through the cell and the fluid flowing through the preceding and following ones.

fig. 1. Combustion furnace scheme

- the flue gas is assumed as a grey body (emissivity independent from radiation wavelength)

- the "Diffusion Hypothesis" is valid for the thermal power calculation exchanged by radiance between the cell fluid and near cell fluid

- the combustion furnace walls are assumed as a grey body with constant emissivity (independent, particularly, from temperature)

- the combustion process is represented as thermal power release corresponding to the complete reaction of the fuel flow rate flowing into the cell

- in the energetical balance related to one cell the source term is only given by the whole thermal power coming from combustion in the cell.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of fluid mass

2) the conservation equation of fluid energy

3) the equation of radiative thermal exchange between two fluids

4) the equation of radiative thermal exchange between one fluid and one surface

5) the equation of convective thermal exchange between one fluid and one surface

6) the partial pressure calculation equation for oxygen (O2) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

7) the partial pressure calculation equation for carbon dioxide (CO2) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

8) the partial pressure calculation equation for sulphur dioxin (SO2) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

9) the partial pressure calculation equation for water (H2O) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet

10) the mass fraction calculation equation for water (H2O) present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet.

Further analyses related to physic-mathematical model development are available in the section concerning this module of the manual "LEGOCAD-Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants-Theory".

Note 1

Because of software limits regarding the solution of the whole equation system (maximum number of equations for each module) it needs that the exchanging surfaces do not exceed 51, even if each cell can exchange heat with 9 surfaces having different temperatures.

Note 2

If the combustion furnace to simulate is equipped with NOx - ports, the cells which are associated to the NOx - ports must be considered as equipped with burners. User must assign to these cells zero fuel flow rate.

Topological data

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

NCEL = cells total number

NPi = number of exchanging surfaces in the i-th cell

NBRUC = number of cells equipped with burners

i = 1, 2, ... NCEL

j = 1, 2, ... NPi

k = 1, 2, ... NBRUC

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[K] TF_i flue gas temperature in the i-th cell
[W/m²/K] GCij equivalent thermal exchange coefficient between flue gas and the j-th surfaces of the i-th cell (see note 3)
[kg/s] WFCC total flow rate of flue gas flowing out from the combustion furnace
[%] %O2 partial pressure of oxygen present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet
[%] %CO2 partial pressure of carbon dioxide present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet
[%] %H2O partial pressure of water present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet
[%] %FMA mass fraction of water present in the flue gas at the combustion furnace outlet
[K] TMij temperature of the j-th exchanging surface in the i-th cell
[kg/s] WCOk fuel flow rate to the k-th burner
[kg/s] WARk air and blending flow rate to the k-th burners
[deg] ABRk training arc of k-th burner as regards to a horizontal plain
[kg/s] WRIF flow rate of recirculating flue gas supplied into the combustion furnace
[K] TRIF temperature of recirculating flue gas supplied into the combustion furnace
[K] TACO temperature of comburent air supplied into the combustion furnace
[ ] FACS corrective factor of equivalent thermal exchange coefficient (see note 3)
[kg/s] WARC total flow rate of comburent air supplied into the combustion furnace
[ ] ETAC combustion efficiency
[kg/s] WGAT total flow rate of tempering gas supplied into the combustion furnace (see note 4)
[ ] AKFL flame length corrective factor (see note 5).

Note 3

The coefficient of GCij equivalent thermal exchange related to the j-th wall of the i-th cell is calculated as follows:

if FCOij > 1

GCij = [GARij * (TF_i² + TMij²) * (TF_i + TMij) + GACij * FCOij] * FACS

if FCOij < 1

GCij = [GARij * FCOij * (TF_i² + TMij²) * (TF_i + TMij) + GACij] * FACS

where

GARij radiative thermal exchange coefficient

GACij convective thermal exchange coefficient

FCOij corresponding to the data FCO(J) C(i)

The FACS is a corrective factor of thermal exchange coefficient which can be identified in case user has to determine stationary conditions where quantities like the exchanged power or the flue gas temperature in a given cell are known.

Note 4

The WGAT variable (total flow rate of tempering gas supplied into the combustion furnace) represents a module optional input. During topology building, it is asked to user if, in the combustion furnace model, a tempering gas contribution is foreseen.

If answer is affirmative the WGAT flow rate appears among the module variables and included among the input variables; otherwise the WGAT flow rate is not considered.

Note 5

AKFL is a corrective factor of flame length. In case of training arcs different from zero, AKFL can be identified when user has to determine the stationary conditions in which some quantities like the exchanged power or the flue gas temperature in some cells are known.

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

NCEL = cells total number

NPi = number of exchanging surfaces in the i-th cell

i = 1, 2, ... NCEL

j = 1, 2, ... NPi

k = 1, 2, ... N.CE.BR.

 

[ ] NCEL number of cells in the combustion furnace scheme
[ ] N.CE.BR. (max. 6.) number of cells equipped with burners
[ ] PARACOMB

parameter for fuel type choosing:

0 = gas
1 = fuel oil
2 = coal
[m] DIAMPART coal particles mean diameter
[kg/m³] ROCARBON coal density
[s] COMBTIME characteristic time of coal complete combustion
[ ] SOLIDFRA burnable volumetric fraction of fuel solid part
[ ] EMISPAR emissivity of combustion furnace walls
[ ] ITMPRING

parameter indicating if tempering gas is flowing (or not) into the combustion furnace:
for ITMPRING > 0.there is tempering gas flowing into the combustion furnace; the index value identifies the cell where tempering gas is flowing into
for ITMPRING < 0. (or = 0.)there is not tempering gas flowing into the combustion furnace
[J/kg] PCI COMB fuel net heat value
[ ] IBRUC(k) corresponding index between cells and burners (see note 6)
[ ] CARBONIO mass fraction of carbon present in the fuel
[ ] IDROGENO mass fraction of hydrogen present in the fuel
[ ] ZOLFO mass fraction of sulphur present in the fuel
[ ] AZOTO mass fraction of nitrogen present in the fuel
[ ] OSSIGENO mass fraction of oxygen present in the fuel
[ ] UMIDCOMB mass fraction of water present in the fuel (fuel humidity)
[ ] UMIDARIA  mass fraction of water present in the comburent air (air humidity)
[1/ft/K] EMLUM K absorption factor of flame luminous emissivity (see note 3)
[kg/s] WNCO C (k) nominal fuel flow rate to the k-th burner
[ ] SOOT C (k) solid particles volumetric concentration (for fuel oil and coal)
[J/kg] EN.COMB fuel specific enthalpy

Data concerning each cell:

[m] LUNG (i) cell length
[m²] SEZ (i) cross section area of cell outlet
[m²] SU (j) C (i) exchange surface area of the j-th cell
[ ] FCO(j) C (i)

if FCO(j) C (i) > 1: ratio between the real surface licked by fluid and the radiated surfaces for the j-th wall;
if FCO(j) C (i) < 1: ratio between the radiated surface and the real surface licked by fluid for the j-th wall

If several consecutive cells have the same data, it is enough to assign those concerning the first cell. The preceding cell data are automatically assigned to the following cells.

Note 6

The simulation foresees a maximum of 6 cells equipped with burners. In the IBRUC_(k) fields user specifies, in a progressive order, the number of cell equipped with burners. If the number of equipped cells is lower than the maximum allowed, the remaining fields must be empty. E.g.: if the cells' total number is 6 and the cells 2 and 3 are only equipped with burners, user must indicate:

IBRUC_1 =2., IBRUC_2 =3., IBRUC_(k=3,4,5,6) =0.

It is an error to indicate: IBRUC_1 =3., IBRUC_2 =2.

MODULE CCMP

CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

For the cells equipped with burners, user must indicate the total air flow rate flowing into the cell, and the fuel total flow rate related to the lighted burners present in the cell.

For the remaining variables no particular prescription is required.

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the module mathematical model give rise to a set of residuals of which only the respective normalization factors are indicated.

Further explanations are given in the manual section: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".

i = 1, 2, ... NCEL

RNI(i)  normalization factor = W0*H0
RNI(NCEL+i) normalization factor = 1000.
RNI(2*NCEL+i) normalization factor = 1000.
RNI(3*NCEL+1) normalization factor = W0
RNI(3*NCEL+2) normalization factor = 1000.
RNI(3*NCEL+3) normalization factor = 1000.
RNI(3*NCEL+4) normalization factor = 1000.
RNI(3*NCEL+5) normalization factor = 1000.
