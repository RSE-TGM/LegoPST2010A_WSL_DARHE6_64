MODULE COAF

CARD A

Model Generalities

AIR AXIAL COMPRESSOR

 

The COAF module simulates a gas turbine axial compressor with the following features:

- the "choke flow" phenomenon is taken into account;

- the "surge" conditions are identified;

- the isentropic efficiency is depending on the compression ratio;

- a bypass valve is used during the start up to reduce the delivery air flow rate and consequently keep lower the pressure inside the furnace.

The air thermodynamic characteristics are obtained by calling the air and flue gas tables (TAVARFUM.FOR), based on the statistical theory of perfect gases, and considering the specific heat of each gas constituent the mixture as dependent on the temperature.

In the module air is treated as a real gas; the calculated results, therefore, depend on the relations utilized in the called tables (that means that real gases could be actually used by means of a simple substitution of the TAVARFUM tables with other ones at user's choice).

[K] TBYP Air temperature upstream the compressor by-pass valve
[kg/s] WBYP Air flow rate through the compressor by-pass valve
[W] QCOM Mechanical power absorbed by the compressor
[p.u.] APER IGV opening degree (see note 1)

Note 1

IGV (Inlet Guide Vanes) opening is assigned as opening angle in degree for the FIAT gas turbine, as servomotor position for the TurboTecnica gas turbine and as opening factor in percentage for any other different compressor.

The XIGV input variable typical values are:

FIAT gas turbine XIGV = 0 for full-load operating

XIGV = 38 for self-sustaining operating

XIGV = 22 for start-up operating

TurboTecnica gas turbine XIGV = 90 for full-load operating

XIGV = 38 for self-sustaining operating

XIGV = 0 for start-up operating

Any other gas turbine XIGV = 100 for full-load operating

XIGV = 82 for self-sustaining operating

XIGV = 70 for start-up operating

The APER (IGV opening degree) output variable represents a flow rate multiplying coefficient and its value is 1 when IGV are fully open. It is evaluated on the basis of the XIGV input variable value and of the type of considered gas turbine.

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of COAF module input and output variables, user must assign the following data set to complete the module input on the file F14:

[kg/s] W nom. Compressor outlet air flow rate in nominal conditions
[Pa] Pi nom. Compressor inlet pressure in nominal conditions
[Pa] Pu nom. Compressor outlet pressure in nominal conditions
[K] Ti nom. Compressor inlet air temperature in nominal conditions
[rad/s] Omeg nom Compressor rotation velocity in nominal conditions
[ ] KpBypass Coefficient defining the pressure upstream the compressor by-pass valve (see note 2)
[ ] Csi nom. Reciprocal of compressor efficiency in nominal conditions
[ ] Coeff.K1 First coefficient of the compressor efficiency variation curve as a function of load (see note 3)
[ ] Coeff.K2 Second coefficient of the compressor efficiency variation curve as a function of load (see note 3)
[ ] Tipo TG

TurboCompressor set type:
Tipo TG = 1. FIAT TG50D e TC50C
Tipo TG = 2. TurboTecnica MS9001
Tipo TG = 3. Any other compressor
[ ] Coeff.AA First coefficient of the air flow rate variation curve as a function of IGV position (see note 4)
[ ] Coeff.BB Second coefficient of the air flow rate variation curve as a function of IGV position (see note 4)
[ ] Coeff.B1 First coefficient of the choke flow curve (see note 5)
[ ] Coeff.B2 Second coefficient of the choke flow curve (see note 5)
[ ] Coeff.A0 First coefficient of the flow rate curves (see note 5)
[ ] Coeff.A2 Second coefficient of the flow rate curves (see note 5)
[ ] Coeff.A3 Third coefficient of the flow rate curves (see note 5)

Note 2

Pressure at the bypass valve is equal to the induction pressure added with a fraction of the drop pressure:

PBYP = PING + KpBypass * (PUSC - PING)

Typical value for KpBypass: 0.5

Note 3

The compressor efficiency varies with the law:

Csi = CsiNom * (AK1 + (1. - AK1) * (1. + AK2 * (1. - X) ** 2.))

where X is the compression ratio in p.u.:

X = ((PUSC - PING) / PING) / ((PuNom - PiNom) / PiNom)

Note 4

For FIAT compressor, AA and BB are two angles in degree (default values: AA = 40. and BB = 66.75) of the law:

APER = (cos(AA) + sin(BB)) / (cos(AA - XIGV) + sin(BB))

For TurboTecnica compressor they are coefficients of a parabola (default values: AA = 0.02896 and BB = 0.28629) depending on XXX = (1. - XIGV / 90):

APER = 1. - AA * XXX ** 2. - BB * XXX

For any other compressor values of AA and BB are not utilized:

APER = XIGV / 100.

Note 5

The choke flow curve is given by:

XCF = B1 * Y + B2 * Y ** 2.

where Y is the blade Mach number in p.u. (see note 3 for the meaning of X).

The flow rate curves (as functions of X and Y) are given by the relations:

choke flow (X <= XCF): Z = A0

elsewhere (X > XCF): Z = A0 + A2 * (X - XCF)². + A3 * (X - XCF)³.

where Z is the flow number in p.u. (default values: B1 = -5., B2 = 6.1111, A0 = 1., A2 = -0.405 and A3 = 0.).

MODULE COAF

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the COAF module mathematical model (mentioned above) give rise to a set of residuals. Here only their correspondences with the output variables equations have been indicated, also mentioning the respective normalization factors. Further explanations are given in the manual section: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory - Modules Description".

1) RNI(1) - normalization factor = W0

related to the compressor air flow rate;

2) RNI(2) - normalization factor = T0

related to the compressor delivery air temperature;

3) RNI(3) - normalization factor = P0

related to the bypass air pressure;

4) RNI(4) - normalization factor = T0

related to the bypass air temperature;

5) RNI(5) - normalization factor = W0

related to the bypass air flow rate during start-up;

6) RNI(6) - normalization factor = W0 * H0

related to the mechanical power absorbed by the compressor;

7) RNI(7) - normalization factor = 1.

related to the IGV opening coefficient.
