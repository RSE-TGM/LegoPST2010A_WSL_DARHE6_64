MODULE LJUN

CARD A

Model Generalities

LJUNGSTRÖM TYPE AIR PREHEATER

 

The LJUN module simulates the dynamics of thermal exchange between air and flue gas in a Ljungström type air preheater. These preheaters are constituted by an iron sheet vertical cylinder subdivided into sectors. In each sector mill packs, arranged so as to realize a large exchange surface between fluid (flue gas or air) and metal, are inserted.

A part of the sectors (generally one half) is crossed lengthwise by the flue gas, the remaining part is crossed by the air, in countercurrent with respect to the flue gas flow. The cylinder is kept in rotation at an angular velocity of 0.1 rad/s - 0.2 rad/s. As a result of the rotation, the mill packs of the same sector are crossed first by the flue gas, from which heat is absorbed, and then by the air that gets the heat previously taken away from flue gas.

In the mathematical model implemented in LJUN module the cylinder is assumed to be subdivided into an even number of sectors and, at a given time "t", one half of the sectors is supposed to be crossed by the flue gas and the other half by the air.

Considering a cylindrical coordinates frame of reference with the "z" axis coinciding with the axis of rotation of the sheet iron cylinder, the temperature distribution in the metal of the mill packs contained in each sector is assumed as uniform both along the "z" axis and in the radial direction.

Besides, treating the thermal exchange between the fluid (flue gas or air) flowing through a given sector and the mill packs metal, the angular distribution of the metal temperature and the axial distribution of the fluid temperature along the sector are both approximated by means of a weighted average of the temperature at the considered sector extremes (in the angular direction and in the axial direction, respectively).

In particular, the average temperature TM_j of the mill packs metal contained in the sector "j" is given by the relation:

TM_j = ALAM * TU_j-1 + (1-ALAM) * TU_j (1)

where ALAM is the weight factor and TU_j the average temperature (in the radial direction and along the "z" axis) of the mill packs metal at the separation surface between the sectors "j" and "j+1" (see fig. 1).

fig.1 Metal temperatures in the cylindrical heater sectors

Moreover, the average temperature Tf_j of the fluid (air or flue gas) in the sector "j" is given by the relation:

Tf_j = ALAM * Ti_j + (1-ALAM) * Tu_j (2)

where ALAM is the same weight factor used for the metal average temperature, while Ti_j and Tu_j are the inlet and outlet fluid temperatures of the sector "j", respectively.

Further hypotheses of the model are:

- negligible mass accumulation in the flue gas as well as in the air;

- uniform flue gas flow rate through all the sectors crossed by the flue gas;

- uniform air flow rate through all the sectors crossed by the air;

- uniform flue gas inlet specific enthalpy over the whole section of each sector crossed by the flue gas;

- uniform air inlet specific enthalpy over the whole section of each sector crossed by the air;

- negligible kinetic and gravitational terms in the energy conservation equations both of the flue gas and of the air.

The following equations have been used:

1) conservation equation of flue gas mass;

2) conservation equation of flue gas energy;

3) conservation equation of air mass;

4) conservation equation of air energy;

5) conservation equation of energy in the mill packs metal.

The first four of the equations above are algebraical, whereas the last one is differential (being associated to a state variable).

With the introduction of the weighted averages already cited, the approximation degree of the solutions determined by the module is depending both on the weight factor ALAM value and on the sectors (angular and axial) dimensions (see: Theory - GENERAL PRINCIPLES - par. I.1.8.4 "The problem of the averages definition in the lumped parameters conservation equations").

On the basis of the acquired experience in developing the Brindisi Sud plant model, a subdivision of the heater into at least four angular sectors and into at least three axial cells is suggested, while a weight factor ALAM equal to 0.4 is advised.

Finally, we note that, since an axial nodalization has not been foreseen in the physical-mathematical model, a single application of the module represents a single axial cell. Therefore, generally, to obtain "m" axial cells "m" LJUN modules must be connected in series.

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[kg/s] WFLJ flue gas total flow rate crossing the heater
[kg/s] WALJ air total flow rate crossing the heater
[K] TG_1, ..., TG_n/2 inlet flue gas temperature in the sector 1, 2, ..., n/2
[K] TI_n/2+1 ... TI_n inlet air temperature in the sector n/2+1, n/2+2, ..., n
[rad/s] OMLJ rotation velocity of the metal cylinder containing the mill packs
[ ] CSCA correction factor of the thermal exchange coefficient between the fluid crossing the heater and the mill packs of the heater
[K] TM_1, ..., TM_n metal average temperature of the mill packs contained in the sector 1, 2,..., n (see above)
[K] TU_1, ..., TU_n metal average temperature (in the radial and axial directions) of the mill packs at the separation surface between two subsequent sectors (see above)
[K] TF_1, ..., TF_n/2 outlet flue gas temperature of the sector 1, 2, ..., n/2
[K] TA_n/2+1 ... TA_n outlet air temperature of the sector n/2+1, n/2+2, ..., n

MODULE LJUN

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of LJUN module input and output variables (meanings defined above), user must assign the following data set to complete the module input on the file F14:

[J/kg/K] CM specific heat of the heater metal
[kg/m³] ROM density of the heater metal
[m³] VMET volume of the heater metal
[m²] ASCA area of the heater total thermal exchange surface
[m] RALJ radius of the metal cylinder containing the mill packs
[ ] ALAM weight factor used to define both the metal average temperature of the mill packs and the average temperature of the fluid (flue gas or air) crossing the different sectors (see above; usual value ALAM = 0.4)
[m²] ALIB area of the fluid (air and flue gas) total cross section in the heater
