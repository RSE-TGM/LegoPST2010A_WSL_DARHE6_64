MODULE CPAF

CARD A

Model Generalities

GAS TURBINE SET COMBUSTION CHAMBER

 

The CPAF module simulates a gas turbine set combustion chamber with the following features:

- two control volumes, related to the precombustion cold chamber and the combustion hot chamber, in which the energy and mass accumulation phenomena are neglected;

- two pressure nodes corresponding to the control volumes;

- a grid between pre-chamber and combustion chamber to increase the flow turbulence;

- the fuel feed system dynamics is represented through a zero / pole transfer function;

- the combustion delay is taken into account by means of a first order transfer function;

- the thermal exchange between the hot chamber metallic walls and the internal flue gas, as well as that one with the external environment are represented.

The thermodynamic characteristics of the two involved fluids (compressed air and flue gas flowing out of the combustion chamber) are obtained by calling the air and flue gas tables (TAVARFUM.FOR), based on the statistical theory of perfect gases, and considering the specific heat of each gas constituent the mixture as dependent on the temperature.

In the module all the gases are treated as real gases; the calculated results, therefore, depend on the relations utilized in the called tables (that means that real gases could be actually used by means of a simple substitution of the TAVARFUM tables with other ones at user's choice).

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

[Pa] PUSC Hot chamber flue gas pressure (turbine suction pressure)
[kg/s] WAIN Combustion chamber inlet air flow rate
[K] TAIN Combustion chamber inlet air temperature
[kg/s] WCIN

Combustion chamber inlet requested fuel flow rate
[J/kg] HCIN Fuel thermal enthalpy
[p.u.] AKPC Pre-chamber / chamber discharge coefficient
[K] TAMB  External environment temperature
[K] TMET  Combustion chamber metallic wall average temperature
[kg/s] WFUM Combustion chamber outlet flue gas flow rate
[K] TFUM Combustion chamber outlet flue gas temperature
[Pa] PING Combustion chamber inlet air pressure
[ ] XCFU  Combustion chamber outlet flue gas chemical composition (after bypass air mixing) defined by the mass ratio between fuel and combustion air (equal to zero if burners are off and air only is flowing out)

MODULE CPAF

CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

In addition to initial values of CPAF module input and output variables (meanings defined above), user must assign the following data set to complete the module input on the file F14:

[J/kg] H combus Fuel net heat value (at 0 °C)
[ ] Eta comb

Combustion efficiency
[ ] nH/nC Hydrogen / Carbon molar ratio in the fuel; it must be lying between 0 (only carbon) and 4 (pure methane): typical values: 3.84 (3.75 - 3.90) commercial methane; 2.15 (2.13 - 2.18) commercial fuel oil
[s] Tps1 Fuel feed system first characteristic time constant (at the numerator of the zero/pole representing its dynamics)
[s] Tps2 Fuel feed system second characteristic time constant (at the denominator of the zero/pole representing its dynamics)
[s] Rit.Comb Combustion delay characteristic time constant
[m²] Sup Int Combustion chamber metallic wall internal surface
[m²] Sup Est Combustion chamber metallic wall external surface
[J/kg/K] Cp Met Combustion chamber wall metal specific heat
[kg] Mass Met Combustion chamber wall metal mass

MODULE CPAF

CARD E 

User's Subprograms

No user subprograms are present in the CPAF module.

The CPAF module makes use of the procedures contained in the air and flue gas thermodynamic tables (TAVARFUM) which must be present in the user library.

MODULE CPAF

CARD F 

Residuals

The equations forming the CPAF module mathematical model give rise to a set of residuals. Here only their correspondences with the output variables equations have been indicated, also mentioning the respective normalization factors. Further explanations are given in the manual section: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory - Modules Description".

1) RNI(1)

- in steady-state conditions:

normalization factor = T0

related to energy conservation inside the metallic wall

- in transient conditions:

normalization factor = T0

related to the metallic wall temperature time derivative

2) RNI(2) - normalization factor = W0

related to the combustion chamber outlet flue gas flow rate;

3) RNI(3) - normalization factor = T0

related to the combustion chamber outlet flue gas temperature;

4) RNI(4) - normalization factor = P0

related to the combustion chamber inlet air pressure;

5) RNI(5) - normalization factor = 0.02

related to the combustion chamber outlet flue gas chemical composition.
