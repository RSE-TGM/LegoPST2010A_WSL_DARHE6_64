MODULE MVAM CARD A

Model Generalities

 

METHANE AND STEAM MIXER

 

The target of the MVAM module is the evaluation of the total flow rate and the equilibrium temperature of two mixing flows, one of (surheated) steam and the other of methane (or natural gas). The target of the module is also to allow the connection between a steam pipe line and a gas pipe, as the definition of the thermodynamical characteristics of the fluid are different for the two types of fluid.

 

Simplifying hypotheses adopted in the physical-mathematical model definition are the following:

- absence of thermal exchange of the "N" fluids with the surface of the cavity;

- absence of phenomena of mass and energy accumulation in the cavity;

 

The hypothesis that the steam is (before and after the mixing) surheated allows the usage (also for the steam) of relations valid for perfect gases. Actually the methane is usually colder than the steam, and it would cause a (partial) condensation of the steam. But one of the effects of the mixing is that the partial pressure of the steam diminuishes greatly (keeping constant the static pressure of the mixture) with the consequence of an equivalent diminution of the condensation temperature.

 

In case of null (or negative) flow rates, the module keeps the chemical composition in the previous time step; during steady state evaluation it is instead kept the attempt value assigned by the user (read from the F14.DAT file).

 

The temperature of the incoming steam is evaluated with a simple call to the steam properties tables of the LEGOCAD System. The partial pressure of the steam is evaluated multiplying the pressure in the mixer for the molar fraction of steam.

MODULE MVAM CARD F

 

The mass and energy conservation equations and the mass balance between methane and steam, applied to the fluid inside the manifold, give rise to the following residuals:

 

WOUT = WSTE + WGAS

IF (WSTE .GE. 0.01 .OR. WGAS .GE. 0.01) THEN

WSTE = AMAX1 (WSTE, 0.)

WGAS = AMAX1 (WGAS, 0.)

HOUT = (WSTE * HSSS + WGAS * HGAS) / (WSTE + WGAS)

XOUT = WGAS / (WSTE + WGAS)

ELSE

HOUT = HPRE

XOUT = XPRE

ENDIF

C

RNI(1) = (WMIX - WOUT) / W0

RNI(2) = (HMIX - HOUT) / H0

RNI(3) = XGAS - XOUT
