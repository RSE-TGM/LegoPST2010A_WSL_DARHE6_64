MODULE CAVE CARD A

Model Generalities

 

CAVITY WITH STEAM AND WATER IN EQUILIBRIUM

 

 

The CAVE module belongs to the thermodinamic base library.

It simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena in a cavity containing water and steam tending to separate each other under the force of gravity effect. Such a module is commonly used to simulate plant components like:

- steam drums of boiler with natural or forced circulation;

- drainage collecting and expansion tanks and flash tanks;

- tanks usually containing water in liquid and steam phases.

In the above-mentioned components, having a very similar purpose, a water-steam mixture flows in. The mixture is separated by means of gravity or centrifugal forces.

Steam and water conditions at the cavity outlet are imposed as identical to the nominal ones (total separation efficiency).

At present the separation model cannot be modified by means of user's subprograms.

In order to use the CAVE module in building a circulation loop model (natural or forced), to define input and output variables, the following items have been taken into account:

- the outlet of liquid towards the circulation loop

- the inlet of liquid-steam mixture coming from the circulation loop

- the inlet of subcooled feedwater.

In the mathematical model it is presumed that the entering subcooled liquid is uniformly mixed with all the liquid present in the cavity.

Further characteristics of the adopted mathematical model are:

- the liquid-steam phases are in thermodynamic equilibrium;

- the liquid volume is in saturated conditions;

- the steam volume is in saturated conditions;

- the thermal exchanges between the internal fluid and the cavity walls are not considered;

- the cavity has constant horizontal cross section area and total volume characterized by the data required in input.

The used equations are:

1) the conservation equation of mass (water + steam) in the cavity

2) the conservation equation of energy (water + steam) in the cavity

3) the equation for saturated steam enthalpy calculation

4) the equation for saturated water enthalpy calculation

5) the equation for pressure calculation on the cavity bottom.

The first two equations are differential whereas the other ones are algebraical.

It is useful to note that CAVE module is able to simulate a situation in which only one of the two phases is present in the cavity. So, it is possible to determine steady-state conditions and transients in which the cavity is only filled by water or steam.

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

 

[kg/s] WLIN flow rate of subcooled water at the cavity inlet

[J/kg] HLIN enthalpy of subcooled water at the cavity inlet

[kg/s] WMXI flow rate of water-steam mixture at the cavity inlet

[J/kg] HMXI enthalpy of water-steam mixture at the cavity inlet

[kg/s] WLUS flow rate of saturated water flowing out from the cavity

[kg/s] WVUS flow rate of saturated steam flowing out from the cavity

[Pa] PCAV pressure in the cavity

[m] LCAV water level in the cavity

[J/kg] HVSA enthalpy of saturated steam flowing out from the cavity

[J/kg] HLSA enthalpy of saturated water flowing out from the cavity

[Pa] PFCA pressure on the cavity bottom

 

 

MODULE CAVE CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

 

 

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, the user must assign the following data:

[m³] VOLTOT cavity internal total volume (steam volume + water volume)

[m²] SEZLIQ horizontal cross section area of the cavity

 

To determine the liquid level, the CAVE module refers to a cavity whose liquid volume below the measuring instrument nozzles is known and the level variation is proportional to the liquid volume variation. So it is a cavity where, within the working range, the horizontal cross section area is constant.

MODULE CAVE CARD F

Residuals

 

The equations forming the module mathematical model give rise to the following residuals for the calculation of the output variables (PCAV, LCAV; HVSA, HLSA, PFCA):

RNI(1) = dPCAV/dt = (F-D*E/B)/(C-D*A/B) (normalization factor = P0)

RNI(2) = dLCAV/dt = (F-C*E/A)/(D-C*B/A) (normalization factor = 1)

RNI(3) = HVSA - HVSAT (normalization factor = H0)

RNI(4) = HLSA - HLSAT (normalization factor = H0)

RNI(5) = PFCA - PFCC (normalization factor = P0)

where:

- HVSAT = HVSAT(PCAV) enthalpy of saturated steam in the cavity

- HLSAT = HLSAT(PCAV) enthalpy of saturated water in the cavity

- ROVS = ROVS(PCAV) density of saturated steam in the cavity

- ROLS = ROLS(PCAV) density of saturated water in the cavity

- PFCC = PCAV + g*ROLS*LCAV pressure on the cavity bottom

- g = 9.80665 m/s² gravity acceleration

and:

- A = VV*DROVSDP + VL*DROLSDP

- B = (ROLS-ROVS)*SEZLIQ

- C = VV*(ROVS*DHVSDP+HVSAT*DROVSDP-1) + VL*(ROLS*DHLSDP+HLSAT*DROLSDP-1)

- D = (ROLS*HLSAT - ROVS*HVSAT)*SEZLIQ

- E = WLIN+WMXI-WLUS-WVUS

- F = WLIN*HLIN + WMXI*HMXI - WLUS*HLSAT - WVUS*HVSAT

with:

- VL = LCAV*SEZLIQ water volume in the cavity

- VV = VOLTOT - VL steam volume in the cavity

- DROVSDP = dROVS/dPCAV derivative of the saturated steam density with regard to the cavity pressure

- DROLSDP = dROLS/dPCAV derivative of the saturated water density with regard to the cavity pressure

- DHVSDP = dHVSAT/dPCAV derivative of the saturated steam enthalpy with regard to the cavity pressure

- DHLSDP = dHLSAT/dPCAV derivative of the saturated water enthalpy with regard to the cavity pressure

The physical meanings of remaining variables have already been defined above.

Further explanations about meanings of the above-mentioned quantities are available in the section concerning the CAVE module of the manual: "LEGOCAD - Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".
