MODULE CLAV CARD A

Model Generalities

 

STEAM-WATER TANK WITH AIR

 

The module CLAV simulates the dynamics of thermo-hydraulic phenomena in a cavity containing water and steam separated by gravity effects. Air inlet and outlet is explicitly considered.

The module is commonly used to simulate plant components like:

- steam drums of boiler with natural or forced circulation

- "upper plenum" of PWR steam generators

- "upper plenum" of BWR

- drainage collecting, expansion tanks and flash tanks

- tanks usually containing water in liquid and steam phases

The following mass exchanges between cavity volume and the external systems have been condidered:

liquid-steam mixture coming from the risers

subcooled water from the feed system

liquid outlet to downcomers

steam outlet to superheating system

air entering through a venting valve, whose stem position is an input of the module

The following hypotheses are set for the module:

air is considered as a perfect gas:

air and steam are always in thermal equilibrium with each other: that means that the air brings itself instantly to the steam temperature:

air and steam occupy the same volume:

air doesn’t interact with the zone occupied by the liquid: there is no air mass diffusion in liquid zone

The main equations of the module are the following ones :

1) air mass conservation

2) steam mass conservation

3) energy conservation for the zone occupied by steam and air

4) liquid mass conservation

5) liquid energy conservation

6) venting valve efflux law

7) liquid level calculation

8) pressure calculation on the cavity bottom.

 

The first five equations are differential whereas the other ones are algebraical.

The equation 7), related to the level calculation, is formulated for a cavity having constant horizontal cross section area characterized by the data required in input (see below). If the cavity has not constant horizontal cross section area, user can assign again the equation 7) by means of a suitable subprogram (see below).

MODULE CLAV CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

 

In addition to the initial values of input and output variables, user must assign the following data:

[] TYPE_CAV selection index about type of cavity to deal with (see below)

[m³] VOL.CAV cavity internal volume (steam + liquid volume)

[] MISURA_Y selection index about the type of level measurement

MISURA_Y = 1. the level is determined as elevation measure of the liquid surface, also including the steam bubbles as inclusion in liquid phase.

MISURA_Y = -1.the level is determined as elevation measure that liquid surface would have excluding the steam bubbles in liquid phase.

[%] X_OVER nominal ratio of liquid-steam mixture flowing out from the separators at cavity top: carry over (typical value 99. < X_OVER < 100.)

[%] X_UNDER nominal ratio of liquid-steam mixture flowing out from the separators at cavity bottom: carry under (typical value X_UNDER = 0.0)

[m] ZERO_Y elevation gap between the liquid level corresponding to the zero of measuring instrument and the water outlet pipe

[m³] VOL_Y cavity volume between the zero of level measuring instrument and the water outlet pipe

[m] GAMMA_Y working range of the level measuring instrument

[m²] SEZ_Y horizontal cross section area of the cavity

[] CV_SF venting valve dimensioning coefficient

 

To determine the liquid level, the CLAV module refers to a cavity whose liquid volume below the measuring instrument nozzles is known and the level variation is proportional to the liquid volume variation. So it is a cavity where within the working range the horizontal cross section area is constant. If a tank having a more complex geometry is considered, user can provide the related data through the user's subprogram

SUBROUTINE CLAVLIV(TYP, VLIV, Y, YTOT, AREA)

(see below). Knowing the engaged volume this subprogram calculates:

- the liquid level as a function of the cavity geometrical characteristics

- the proportional level of liquid as a function of the measuring instruments nozzles characteristic

- the area of separating surface between the liquid and steam phases.

In this case, user has to assign to the SEZ_Y a negative or zero value.

If this signal is found, the user subprogram is called and values of ZERO_Y, VOL_Y, GAMMA_Y, and SEZ_Y are ignored.

MODULE CLAV CARD E

User's Subprograms

 

If the CLAV has a more complex geometry than the reference one, to provide the related data to the module, user has to assign a negative or zero value to SEZ_Y and write a user's subprogram whose declarative statement is:

SUBROUTINE CLAVLIV(TYP, VLIV, Y, YTOT, AREA).

Knowing the engaged liquid volume such subprogram calculates:

- the level of liquid as a function of the cavity geometrical characteristics

- the proportional level of liquid as a function of the measuring instruments characteristics

- the area of separating surface between the liquid and steam phases

Input parameters: TYP, VLIV

Output parameters: Y, YTOT, AREA

[] TYP corresponds to the TYPE_CAV datum required in input (see above). It can be used as pointer to select the required cavity geometry in case user has to introduce different models of cavities into only one user subprogram

[m³] VLIV engaged liquid volume

[%] Y liquid level, as percentage of the measuring instruments working

range

[m] YTOT level of liquid inside the cavity

[m²] AREA area of separating surface between liquid and steam phases.

 

Since cylindrical geometry having horizontal axis is one of the most usual geometries for drums, the following subprogram is available in the module:

SUBROUTINE CLAVLIO(VLIV, RCAV, HLCAV, Y, YTOT, AREA),

which calculates (once the liquid volume contained inside an horizontal cylinder has been assigned) the level and area of contact surface between the liquid and steam phases.

The meanings of calling parameters are:

[m³] VLIV volume of liquid inside the cylinder

[m] RCAV cylinder radius

[m] HLCAV cylinder length

[%] Y liquid level, as percentage of the measuring instruments working range

[m] YTOT level of liquid inside the cylinder

[m²] AREA area of separating surface between the liquid and steam phases.

Thereinafter it is indicated a CLAVLIV subroutine example using the CLAVLIO, related to a cavity consisting of a cylindrical drum having radius RCAV = 1 m and length HLCAV = 10 m, positioned on a parallelepiped having volume VOL_Y = 5 m³. The gap ZERO_Y between the zero of level measuring instrument and the pipe nozzle of flowing out liquid is 2 m. The level Y is indicated as percentage as regards to a range GAMMA_Y = 1.6 m centred round the horizontal axis of cylinder.

 

SUBROUTINE CLAVLIV(TYP,VLIV,Y,YTOT,AREA)

IF(TYP.NE.1.) RETURN

GAMMA_Y=1.6

RCAV=1.

HLCAV=10.

VOL_Y=5.

ZERO_Y=2.

VCIL=VLIV-VOL_Y

CALL CLAVLIO(VCIL,RCAV,HLCAV,Y,YTOT,AREA)

Y=(YTOT-(RCAV-GAMMA_Y/2.))/GAMMA_Y

YTOT=YTOT+ZERO_Y

RETURN

END

A "drying" phase (dehumidification) of steam may follow the mixture separation phase. This second phase is typical in steam generators of PWR and BWR nuclear reactors. The mathematical model of CLAV module does not make distinction between the separation and drying phases. In addition, the outlet conditions of steam and liquid from the separators are fixed by assigned input data and considered as boundary conditions.

In order to take into account the thermo-hydraulic conditions influence of mixture, at the cavity inlet, on separation-dehumidification process, user must intervene on the module programming, suitably modifying the subprogram:

SUBROUTINE CLAVSEP(TYP, RXOV, RXUN, XR, WR, P, XOV, XUN).

Input parameters: TYP, RXOV, RXUN, XR, WR, P

Outlet parameters: XOV, XUN

[] TYP corresponds to the TYPE_CAV value required in input (see above). It can be used as pointer to associate to a given geometry of a selected cavity the efficiency of corresponding separation-dehumidification process, in case user has to introduce different models of this efficiency into only one user subprogram

[p.u.] RXOV nominal ratio of liquid-steam mixture flowing out from the separators at cavity top (it corresponds to X_OVER datum assigned in input)

[p.u.] RXUN nominal ratio of liquid-steam mixture flowing out from the separators at cavity bottom (it corresponds to X_UNDER datum assigned in input)

[p.u.] XR enthalpy ratio of mixture at the cavity inlet

[kg/s] WR flow rate of mixture at the cavity inlet

[Pa] P pressure in the cavity

[p.u.] XOV ratio of liquid-steam mixture flowing out from the separators at cavity top

[p.u.] XUN ratio of liquid-steam mixture flowing out from the separators at cavity bottom
