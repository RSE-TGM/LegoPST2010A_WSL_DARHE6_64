MODULE AXCH CARD A

Model Generalities

 

AIR DUCT

 

The AXCH module simulates the thermal exchange between air flowing through a duct and the external surface of several elements which (also belonging to the duct itself) can have mean temperatures considerably different.

Symplifying hypoteses used in definition of physical-mathematical model are the following:

 

- duct which air is flowing through is subdivided in cells (max.9) along motion direction;

- in each cell air can exchange with several surfaces (max.6) having different mean temperatures;

- air can be dry or wet;

- humidity cannot condensate, that is to say steam can be superheated or saturated dry;

- convective thermal exchange can occur as longflow;

- all accumulation phenomena are not considered.

 

The used equations are:

 

- conservation equation of air energy

- conservation equation of air mass

- equation of thermal exchange between air and duct walls .

 

Not considering all accumulation phenomena, all mentioned equations are algebraical.

 

 

MODULE AXCH CARD B

Topological data

 

INPUT-OUTPUT SCHEME

 

Nc = number of cells (max.9) in which the duct is subdivided

Np(i) = number of surfaces (max.6) exchanging heat with air flowing through the

i-th duct cell

 

i = 1, 2,... Nc

j = 1, 2,... Np(i)

 

 

PHYSICAL MEANING OF MODULE INPUT-OUTPUT VARIABLES

  

[K] TUF flue gas temperature at the duct outlet

 

[%] XUMD air absolute humidity (mass fraction)

 

[K] TF_1 (i=1,2,...,Nc) mean temperature of air flowing through the i-th cell

 

[W/m**2/K] GF_1 heat transfer coefficient in the i-th cell

 

[kg/s] WIF flow rate of air flowing through the duct

 

[K] TIF air temperature at the duct inlet

 

[ ] FAS dirty factor of bench pipes

 

[Pa] PRES air pressure

 

[%] XUMA air humidity (mass fraction)

if TYUM = 0 relative humidity

if TYUM = 1 absolute humidity

 

[K] TMji mean temperature of the j-th surface exchanging heat with air flowing

through the i-th cell

 

 

MODULE AXCH CARD C

Physical and Geometrical Data

 

In addition to initial values of the AXCH module input and output variables (meanings defined in card B1), user must assign the following data:

 

[ ] TYUM air humidity definition

TYUM = 0 relative humidity

TYUM = 1 absolute humidity

 

and, for each cell:

[ ] NTUB number of pipes forming the bench

[m] DIDR hydraulic diameter of bench pipes (air side)

[m] LUNG length of bench pipes

[m**2] SCAN cell cross section area

[ ] FA correction factor for heat trasfer coefficient

[m**2] SUP.j (j=1,...,6) area of the j-th surface exchanging heat with air flowing through the cell.

 

 

MODULE AXCH CARD D

Module Variables Initialization

 

To initialize the AXCH module variables no particular prescription is required.

Care must be used in assigning realistic values of inlet air humidity.

MODULE AXCH CARD F

Residuals

 

Equations forming the module mathematical model, mentioned in card A1 give rise to a residual set whose are only indicated their respective normalization factor.

Further explanation are given in the related section of the manual "LEGOCAD -

Modelling Library for Simulation of Power Generation Plants - Theory".
