Electrical modules: synchronous machine (simplified model)

 

Modules referred to: GENE.

The model simulates the behavior of a triphase third order sinchronous generator.

The adopted mathematical model utilizes the following leading hypotheses:

hysteresis and magnetic saturation phaenomena are omitted

field circuit acts along the direct axis

a damping circuit is present along both direct and quadrature axis

magnetomotive forces and fluxes distributions have sinusoidal shapes at the air gap (entrefer)

omopolar circuit is left out

transformatoric electromotive forces are left out

time variation of subtransient electromotive forces are omitted

Electrical modules: asinchronous motor

 

Modules referred to: MASI.

This model simulates the behavior of an asinchronous motor.

A Park representation of an asinchronous machine is used. It can simulate a motor of whatsoever power and having a whatsoever type of statoric and rotoric winding, as it refers itself to an equivalent machine characterized by star windings.

One could determine the electrical parameters of the equivalent circuit used for module solution in the following way:

as operator-fixed basing on experience

calculated from tag-data and nominal mechanical load values

if machine tag parameters are not available the program assigns them automatically on the base of the machine power.

To use correctly the related modules, the base tension of tranformer side it’s connected to must be the same as the nominal tension of the motor itself.

These are the main model hypotheses:

statoric and rotoric windings are symmetrical

a two poles equivalent machine is considered having statoric and rotoric windings star-connected

the rotor is isotrope (induction coefficients don’t depend on the angle position)

mutual induction coefficient vary following a co-sinusoidal law in function of the electrical angle between windings axes

magnetic circuits are linear and iron losses are considered irrelevant during Park transforming (these losses are then considered by introducing subsequently in the Park equivalent circuit an equivalent resistence in parallel at the inlet terminal)

time derivative of statoric flux is omitted (this is equivalent to leave out a current unidirectional component appearing during the first instants of a transient)

Electrical modules: transformer with tap changer

 

Modules referred to: TRAF.

This model simulates a triphase variable ratio transformer.

Variable ratio is got by acting on the number of high tension spires. It represents a transformer phase with a gamma double bipole.

Transversal parameters are consituted by iron losses equivalent resistance and magnetization reactance. Longitudinal parameters are short circuit equivalent impedance brought to the primary, whose value depends on the transformation ratio.

With this model it’s possible to simulate a generic transformer, whichever its power and its primary and secondary windings, as it bases itself on an equivalent machine having both windings of star configuration.

Electrical parameters for the equivalent circuit used for model solution are determined from tag data.

Network base voltage must be different for the two transformer sides and equal to the nominal value for both primary and secondary terminal. It’s not allowed to have base tensions which differ from the nominal ones.

In the adopted mathematical model the following hypotheses are assumed:

phase displacement between primary and secondary tension depending on the transformer group is not considered

non-linearities introduced by iron are omitted

a variable ratio transformer is considered in load conditions, with primary winding spires variation

star equivalent of transformer is considered

time derivatives of primary and secondary currents are omitted, that is transients are considered as if at each integration step they attain a sinusoidal regime condition, so to have a pure algebraic model (as a consequency of this hypothesis one can’t observe the transients of switch on from void; these ones can be obtained by introducing the state variables currents and the transformer parasitic capacity together with the tension applied to it)

the equations describing the equivalent circuit are used in the mathematical model of the transformer, containing also the expression of its losses.

two internal switches are introduced in the transformer model by means of some conditions on the equations system

Electrical modules: electrical PI-line model

 

Modules referred to: LINA.

This model simulates the functioning of a triphase line characterized by adjustable lenght, which could be modified by acting on a model variable.

A phase of the line is represented by a pi double bipole. Transversal parameters are constituted by an conductance equivalent to the losses, that are proportional to the second power of the tension, and by the capacitive suceptance of the line, considered as a condenser.

The longitudinal impedence by total resistence and reactance of the line.

The module can simulate a line in which the phaenomenon of waves propagation may be omitted (short lines).

The following hypotheses are adopted in the mathematical model:

the effect of dissimmetry among the phases due to geometrical conductors disposition is left out

waves propagation along the line is left out (sinusoidal regime is hypotized at each time step: hypothesis of short line) (as a consequency of this last hypothesis one can’t observe the transients of switch on from void; these ones can be obtained by introducing the state variables currents together with the tension applied to it)

the equations describing the pi equivalent circuit are used in the mathematical model of the line, containing also the expression of its losses

two internal switches are introduced in the line model by means of some conditions on the equations system

Electrical modules: motor joint for pump or fan (simplified model)

 

Modules referred to: GIPO.

This model simulates the coupling between a motor and an operating machine. It’s used, together with the models of an asynchronous motor and of a generic operating machines (typically a centrifugal pump, an air/gas compressor or a fan).

 

 

Three typical coupling cases are contemplated:

a motor coupled to an operating machine by means of an hydraulic coupling and, contemporarily to another operating machine(e.g. booster pump + motor + feed pump; the booster pump rotates at motor speed while feed pump can vary its speed depending on the value of the input variable ALSC, simulating the behavior of the pump "scoop");

motor coupled to a pump by means of a multiplier or a reducer of gears speed;

motor coupled with a vent by means of a multiplier or a reducer of gears speed: with respect to the previous case, the expression of resistent power changes, depending now on the input ALSC, which represents suction lock-gates position.

In case of rigid coupling all rotate at the same speed, and the mechanical equation is valid:

If the coupling is effectuated by means of a hydraulic coupling gear and a multiplier (or demultiplier of speed), two different speed must be considered. The transmission ratio will be the product of the ones of multiplier and coupling gear.In steady state conditions gear speed is considered linear with the scoop, while in transient conditions a linear relationship is supposed between motor speed and operating machine.
