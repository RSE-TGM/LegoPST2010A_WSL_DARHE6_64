Air and flue gas system components: air heaters

 

A Ljungström air heater is modelled. These heaters are constituted by an iron sheet vertical cylinder subdivided into N sectors. In each sector mill packs, arranged in order to obtain a large exchange surface between fluid (flue gas or air) and metal, are inserted.

A part of the sectors (generally half) is crossed lengthwise by the flue gas, the remaining part is crossed by the air, in countercurrent with respect to the flue gas flow.

The cylinder is kept in rotation at an angular velocity of 0.1 ÷ 0.2 rad/s.

As a result of the rotation, the mill packs of the same sector are crossed first by the flue gas, from which heat is absorbed, and then by the air that gets the heat previously taken away from flue gas.

In the mathematical model the cylinder is assumed to be subdivided into an even number of sectors and, at a given time "t", one half of the sectors is supposed to be crossed by the flue gas and the other half by the air.

Considering a cylindrical coordinates frame of reference with the "z" axis coinciding with the axis of rotation of the sheet iron cylinder, the temperature distribution in the metal of the mill packs contained in each sector is assumed as uniform both along the "z" axis and in the radial direction.

Besides, treating the thermal exchange between the fluid (flue gas or air) flowing through a given sector and the mill packs metal, the angular distribution of the metal temperature and the axial distribution of the fluid temperature along the sector are both approximated by means of a weighted average of the temperature at the considered sector extremes (in the angular direction and in the axial direction, respectively).
