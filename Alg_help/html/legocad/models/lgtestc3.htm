Test case 3: general description

The following test case represents the combustion chamber of a "once through" boiler of a 320 MW thermo-electrical power plant.

The physical phenomena simulated by this model are:

1. combustion of the fuel oil delivered by six groups of burners to the furnace

2. radiative and convective heat exchange between the hot gas and the furnace walls

3. steam production in the walls of the combustion chamber

The combustion chamber is represented by the block CCOM; this block is connected with the blocks PA12 and PA34 (evaporator walls) to simulate the heat transfer from the hot gas to the water circulating in the evaporator walls.

The cell 2 of the block PA12 and the cell 3 of the block PA34, which are not exchanging heat with the flue gas contained in the combustion chamber, represent two intermediate manifolds in the pipe bundles.

The inlet and outlet valves don't represent physical plant components: they are used to simply simulate boundary conditions of the combustion chamber subsystem. In the plant, these conditions are imposed by the feed water subsystem (at the inlet side) and the rest of the boiler and the turbine group (at the outlet side).

The dynamical behaviour of this model has been studied in a case of a 10% reduction of the fuel oil flow rate to the burners.
