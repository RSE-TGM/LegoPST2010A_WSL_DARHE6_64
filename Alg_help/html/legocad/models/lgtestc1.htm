Test case 1: general description

The purpose of the following test case is to represent the HP steam production in a waste heat boiler of a 50 MW dual steam combined cycle plant.

The HP drum is represented by the block S101 (module CLAV); the water circulation (without circulating pumps) and steam generation system is simulated by the blocks E102 (module DRIS), X102 (module METP) and F115-F165 (modules FUMB). Each of these blocks performs a particular function:

E102 represents the thermo-hydraulical water evolution in the water circulation system;

X102 represents the heat transfer through the pipes metal wall;

F115, F125, F135, F145, F155, F155 represent the gas-metal wall heat exchange.

E102 and X102 are divided into 8 cells: the first cell simulate the downcomers; the following 6 cells represent the water evaporator pipes; the last cell represents the return pipes to the drum. Each of the evaporator cells interfaces with a module FUMB to simulate the cross-flow heat exchange between exhaust gas and metal pipes.

The pressure drop in the steam traps of the drum is simulated by the valve V132 (module VALV).

A steam valve (block TURB) is provided to represent, in a simplified way, the steam pressure drop from the drum to the turbine discharge.

The dynamical behaviour of this system is studied in a case of feedwater flow rate increase and temperature reduction.
